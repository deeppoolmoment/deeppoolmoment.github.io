Best Places for Solo Black Female Travel 2024#

2024 is upon us, so gear up and explore the globe. As a solo black female traveler, safety, cultural immersion, and empowerment are your watchwords—and here are seven destinations that deliver.

Cape Town, South Africa:#

  • Malaria-free Mount Table: Table Mountain has cable cars, Table Mountain National Park offers hiking and stunning viewpoints.
  • Beaches Galore: Boulders Beach is a unique beach, home to an African penguin colony.
  • History and Culture: The District Six Museum recounts apartheid-era forced removals, while colorful Bo-Kaap delights with its Cape Malay architecture.
  • Safety: South Africa has areas with high crime rates, but Cape Town has a dedicated tourist police force and generally low risks.

Havana, Cuba:#

  • Salsa Nights: Cuba’s salsa scene is world renowned. Try Casa de la Musica or the iconic Tropicana Club.
  • Carribean Feels: Morro Castle and Castillo de San Carlos de la Cabaña offer a glimpse into Cuba’s colonial past.
  • Hemingway’s Haunts: Visit Hotel Ambos Mundos, Ernest Hemingway’s former residence, and enjoy mojitos at La Bodeguita del Medio.
  • Afro-Cuban Culture: Havana’s rich Afro-Cuban heritage is waiting to be explored.

Lisbon, Portugal:#

  • Seven Hills City: Explore the historic Castelo de São Jorge or catch some sun at Praia de Carcavelos.
  • Culinary Delights: Indulge in pastel de nata, delicious custard tarts, and bacalhau, a salted cod dish.
  • Moorish and Maritime History: Explore Belém Tower and Jerónimos Monastery, symbols of Portugal’s maritime power.
  • Safety: Portugal is a safe destination.

Tulum, Mexico:#

  • Mayan Ruins: Explore the ancient Mayan ruins of Cobá and discover cenotes, natural sinkholes, ideal for swimming.
  • Yoga and Wellness: Tulum’s yoga and wellness scene is thriving, offering retreats and holistic experiences.
  • Beaches: Tulum’s beaches, like Playa Paraíso, offer stunning natural beauty and turquoise waters.
  • Safety: Practice caution in Tulum, as there have been reports of petty crime and drug-related violence.

Accra, Ghana:#

  • Cultural Immersion: Learn about Ghanaian history at the National Museum, explore art at the Ghana National Cultural Centre and find authentic souvenirs at Makola Market.
  • Beaches: Kokrobite Beach is known for surfing and lively beach bars.
  • Vibrant Nightlife: Experience Ghana’s vibrant nightlife at clubs like Republic Bar and Grill or live music venues like Alliance Française d’Accra.
  • Safety: Accra is generally safe, but be vigilant and take precautions against petty crime.

Phnom Penh, Cambodia:#

  • Temples and Palaces: Marvel at the intricate architecture of the Royal Palace and Silver Pagoda.
  • Local Life: Visit the Russian Market, known for textiles and handicrafts, and sample local treats like fish amok at Romdeng.
  • Khmer Culture: The Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum and Choeung Ek Killing Fields provide a glimpse into Cambodia’s tragic past.
  • Safety: Phnom Penh is generally safe, but take precautions against petty crime.

Quito, Ecuador:#

  • Colonial Charm: Explore the historic center, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and admire the beauty of churches like La Compañía de Jesús.

  • Teleférico: Take the Teleférico cable car to Cruz Loma for panoramic city views and hike to the summit of Pichincha Volcano.

  • Local Markets: Otavalo Market, north of Quito, is famous for its colorful textiles and handicrafts.

  • Safety: Quito has areas with high crime, like La Mariscal, so stay vigilant and avoid walking alone at night.

Affordability and Value#

As a seasoned travel writer, I’ve discovered that solo black female travel doesn’t have to break the bank. With careful planning, you can embark on empowering adventures without compromising your budget. Let’s delve into the art of affordable and valuable travel:

Planning and Budgeting:

  • Set a Realistic Budget: Before you start packing your bags, set a realistic budget that aligns with your financial goals and travel aspirations. This will help you make informed decisions about destinations, accommodations, and activities.

  • Consider Off-Season Travel: If you’re flexible with your travel dates, consider exploring destinations during their off-season. This often means lower prices on flights, accommodations, and attractions.

  • Take Advantage of Discounts: Research discounts and deals offered by airlines, hotels, and tour operators. Many destinations offer special rates for students, seniors, and military personnel.

Accommodations:

  • Hostels and Guesthouses: Hostels and guesthouses are excellent options for budget-conscious travelers. They provide shared accommodations and common areas, allowing you to meet other travelers and save money.

  • Budget-Friendly Hotels: Many cities offer budget-friendly hotels that provide basic amenities and a comfortable stay without breaking the bank.

  • Airbnb and Vacation Rentals: Airbnb and vacation rentals can be a cost-effective alternative to hotels, especially if you’re traveling with a group or seeking a more local experience.

Transportation:

  • Public Transportation: Utilize public transportation systems, such as buses, trains, and metros, to get around your destination. They are often inexpensive and convenient.

  • Ridesharing Services: Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft can be a cost-effective way to travel short distances within a city.

  • Consider Walking or Biking: If possible, explore your destination on foot or by bike. Not only will you save money, but you’ll also get some exercise and see the city from a different perspective.

Food:

  • Local Markets and Street Food: Indulge in local markets and street food stalls. They offer authentic culinary experiences at a fraction of the cost of restaurants.

  • Picnics and Home-Cooked Meals: Pack picnics or prepare home-cooked meals to save money on dining out. Many accommodations have kitchens or kitchenettes where you can prepare your meals.

  • Look for Restaurant Deals: Research local restaurants that offer discounts or special promotions, such as happy hour or early bird specials.

Activities and Attractions:

  • Free or Low-Cost Activities: Many destinations offer free or low-cost activities, such as walking tours, museum days, and outdoor concerts.

  • Consider Off-the-Beaten-Path Gems: Explore hidden gems and lesser-known attractions that are often free or have minimal entrance fees.

  • Take Advantage of Tourist Passes: If your destination has a tourist pass, consider purchasing one. It can provide access to multiple attractions at a discounted price.

Cultural Immersion:

  • Engage with Locals: Engage with locals, learn about their culture, and seek out authentic experiences. This can often be done for free or at a minimal cost.

  • Support Local Businesses: Support local businesses by shopping at local markets, eating at local restaurants, and using local tour operators.

Key Takeaways:

  • Plan and set a realistic budget to align with your financial goals and travel aspirations.
  • Consider off-season travel and take advantage of discounts and deals.
  • Choose budget-friendly accommodations like hostels, guesthouses, or vacation rentals.
  • Utilize public transportation, ridesharing services, or explore destinations on foot or by bike.
  • Indulge in local markets and street food, pack picnics, or prepare home-cooked meals.
  • Look for restaurant deals and take advantage of tourist passes for discounted access to attractions.
  • Engage with locals, learn about their culture, and support local businesses for authentic experiences.

For those seeking spiritual enlightenment, Varanasi, India, beckons you with its sacred aura. Varanasi is a city steeped in spirituality, where you can delve into the depths of your soul and connect with your inner self. Wander through the bustling markets, savor the flavors of traditional Indian cuisine, and immerse yourself in the vibrant cultural tapestry of this holy city.

Last but not least, Kyoto, Japan, awaits you with its timeless beauty and rich cultural heritage. Stroll through the enchanting bamboo forests, visit ancient temples, and admire the exquisite geisha districts. Kyoto invites you to experience the essence of Japanese tradition, from the serene tea ceremonies to the delicate art of flower arrangement. Discover hidden gardens, indulge in authentic Japanese cuisine, and let the tranquility of Kyoto soothe your weary soul.
