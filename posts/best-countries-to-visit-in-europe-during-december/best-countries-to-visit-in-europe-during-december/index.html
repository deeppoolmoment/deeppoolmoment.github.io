[The Best Countries to Visit in Europe During December: A Journey Through Festive Charm]

When the holiday season rolls in, Europe transforms into a magical winter wonderland, inviting travelers to experience the joyous spirit of Christmas markets, indulge in thrilling winter sports, partake in unique festive events, and delve into the charm of cultural attractions. Discover the countries that offer the most enchanting December experiences, along with their pros and cons, suggested itineraries, practical tips, and a curated list of must-see attractions, hidden gems, and local traditions to ensure an extraordinary winter journey.

Best Countries to Visit in Europe During December

Deck the halls and prepare for a magical yuletide adventure as we unveil the best countries to visit in Europe during December. From enchanting Christmas markets to snow-capped mountains and fireside festivities, Europe transforms into a winter wonderland during the festive season, offering travelers an unforgettable holiday experience.

Experience the Magic of Christmas Markets

Immerse yourself in the heartwarming tradition of European Christmas markets, a staple of the holiday season. Stroll through the festive stalls adorned with twinkling lights, savor mulled wine and local delicacies, and shop for unique handmade gifts. Whether it’s the Christkindlmarkt in Nuremberg, Germany, or the marché de Noël in Strasbourg, France, each market offers a unique glimpse into the country’s culture and traditions.

Explore Festive Cities Adorned in Holiday Decor

European cities take on a new charm during December, adorned with twinkling lights, festive decorations, and elaborate Christmas displays. Marvel at the grand Christmas tree in Trafalgar Square, London, or wander through the illuminated Champs-Élysées in Paris. Take a carriage ride through Vienna’s Old Town, or glide on ice skates at one of the many outdoor rinks in Copenhagen.

Indulge in Winter Sports and Alpine Adventures

For those seeking an active holiday, Europe’s many ski resorts offer exhilarating slopes and breathtaking scenery. Whether you’re a seasoned skier or a beginner, there are resorts suitable for all levels. From the challenging slopes of the French Alps to the picturesque runs in the Italian Dolomites, indulge in the thrill of gliding down snowy mountains and cozying up by a roaring fire in a charming chalet.

Escape to a Winter Wonderland

Venture beyond the cities to discover Europe’s enchanting winter landscapes. Go snowshoeing through the snowy forests of Finland, embark on a husky safari in the frozen wilderness of Lapland, or take a sleigh ride through the picturesque villages of the Austrian Alps. These unique experiences offer a chance to connect with nature and embrace the tranquility of the winter season.

Celebrate New Year's Eve in Style

Ring in the New Year with a spectacular celebration in one of Europe’s vibrant cities. Join the crowds at the Brandenburg Gate in Berlin for a dazzling fireworks display, dance the night away at a lively party in London, or enjoy a romantic dinner cruise along the River Seine in Paris. As the clock strikes midnight, raise a glass of champagne and make unforgettable memories in a festive atmosphere.

Germany: A Winter Wonderland of Christmas Markets and Cozy Traditions#

  • Must-See Attractions: Immerse yourself in the enchanting ambiance of Nuremberg’s Christkindlesmarkt, Germany’s oldest and most renowned Christmas market, dating back to the 16th century. Explore the festive stalls selling handcrafted ornaments, mulled wine, and gingerbread.
  • Hidden Gems: Venture into the picturesque town of Rothenburg ob der Tauber, a medieval marvel with cobblestone streets, half-timbered houses, and a romantic atmosphere. Don’t miss the chance to visit the Käthe Wohlfahrt Christmas Village, a year-round Christmas wonderland.
  • Local Traditions: Experience the warmth of German hospitality at traditional Christmas markets, where you can indulge in local delicacies like roasted chestnuts, potato pancakes, and stollen, a sweet bread filled with dried fruits and candied citrus peel.

Finland: A Journey into the Arctic Circle for Northern Lights and Snowy Adventures#

  • Must-See Attractions: Marvel at the celestial display of the Northern Lights in Finnish Lapland, a natural phenomenon that paints the sky with vibrant colors. Embark on a husky safari through the snowy wilderness, a thrilling experience that lets you witness the beauty of the Arctic landscape.
  • Hidden Gems: Discover the hidden gem of Levi, a charming ski resort nestled amidst stunning mountains. Take advantage of the excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities, or try your hand at snowshoeing or ice fishing.
  • Local Traditions: Immerse yourself in the Finnish sauna culture, an essential part of the local lifestyle. Experience the unique sensation of alternating between hot and cold temperatures, followed by a refreshing dip in the icy waters of a nearby lake.

Austria: A Festive Symphony of Music, Mountains, and Christmas Traditions#

  • Must-See Attractions: Be mesmerized by the Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra’s New Year’s Concert, a global tradition that fills the Musikverein concert hall with enchanting melodies. Take a romantic horse-drawn sleigh ride through the snow-covered landscapes of Salzburg, the birthplace of Mozart.
  • Hidden Gems: Explore the charming village of Hallstatt, a picturesque gem nestled amidst the Dachstein Mountains. Admire the stunning views of the frozen lake and the surrounding peaks, a sight that resembles a winter postcard.
  • Local Traditions: Indulge in the sweet treat of Salzburger Nockerl, a fluffy soufflé-like dessert topped with powdered sugar, a local delicacy that delights every palate.

Italy: A Cultural Tapestry of Art, History, and Festive Delights#

  • Must-See Attractions: Experience the grandeur of Rome adorned in its festive attire during the Christmas season. Visit the iconic Trevi Fountain, toss a coin, and make a wish while admiring the illuminated streets and decorations. Explore the Uffizi Gallery in Florence, home to masterpieces by Michelangelo, Raphael, and Leonardo da Vinci.
  • Hidden Gems: Uncover the hidden gem of Val Gardena in the Dolomites, a breathtaking valley known for its stunning ski slopes, picturesque villages, and Ladin culture. Enjoy the traditional dishes, such as canederli, dumplings filled with spinach, speck, and cheese.
  • Local Traditions: Embrace the Italian tradition of La Befana, an old woman who brings gifts to children on Epiphany Eve, leaving them in their stockings or shoes. Enjoy the sweet treat of panettone, a traditional Christmas cake filled with candied fruits and nuts, a symbol of the holiday season.

Sweden: A Winter Wonderland of Ice Hotels, Reindeer Safaris, and Festive Traditions#

  • Must-See Attractions: Experience a unique stay at the ICEHOTEL in Jukkasjärvi, a remarkable hotel built entirely from ice and snow, offering an unforgettable Arctic experience. Embark on a reindeer safari through the snowy Lapland wilderness, an adventure that connects you with the indigenous Sami culture.
  • Hidden Gems: Discover the hidden gem of Gamla Stan, Stockholm’s Old Town, a charming historic district with cobblestone streets, colorful buildings, and quaint shops. Immerse yourself in the festive atmosphere of the Stortorget Christmas Market, where you can find traditional Swedish crafts, decorations, and culinary delights.
  • Local Traditions: Embrace the Swedish tradition of Lucia, a festival of lights celebrated on December 13th. Witness the procession of Lucia, a young woman dressed in white and crowned with candles, as she brings light to the darkest days of winter.

Whether it’s the snow-covered streets of Paris or the Christmas markets of Germany, Europe is a magical place to visit in winter. But to make the most of your trip, it’s essential to be prepared for the cold weather and unique challenges that come with traveling during this time.

Here are some practical tips and advice to help you plan your perfect winter getaway to Europe:

Packing Essentials:#

  • Warm clothing: It goes without saying that you’ll need to pack warm clothes for your trip to Europe in the winter. This includes a thick coat, sweater, scarves, hats, gloves, and sturdy boots.
  • Layering: Dressing in layers is key to staying warm in Europe’s cold weather, as it allows you to adjust your clothing to suit the changing temperatures.
  • Thermal underwear: A good set of thermal underwear will help keep you warm even in the coldest weather.
  • Comfortable shoes: You’ll be doing a lot of walking on your European adventure, so make sure to pack comfortable shoes with good traction.
  • Travel-sized toiletries: Pack travel-sized toiletries to save space in your luggage.
  • Universal adapter: If you’re traveling from outside Europe, pack a universal adapter so you can use your electronic devices.

Navigating Transportation:#

  • Public transportation: Europe has an excellent public transportation system, making it easy to get around even in the winter.
  • Trains: Trains are a comfortable and efficient way to travel between major European cities.
  • Buses: Buses are a more budget-friendly option than trains, but they can be slower.
  • Car rental: If you want the freedom to explore at your own pace, consider renting a car. Just be aware that driving conditions can be challenging in the winter.

Staying Safe:#

  • Be prepared for pickpockets: Keep your belongings close to you and be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Be careful in icy conditions: Sidewalks and roads can be icy in the winter, so wear shoes with good traction and be careful when walking.
  • Be aware of the local laws and customs: Make sure to research the local laws and customs before you travel to avoid any misunderstandings.

Key Takeaways:

  • Pack warm clothes, including a thick coat, sweaters, scarves, hats, gloves, and sturdy boots.
  • Dress in layers to adjust to changing temperatures.
  • Wear comfortable shoes with good traction for walking.
  • Pack travel-sized toiletries to save space in your luggage.
  • Use a universal adapter for your electronic devices if you’re traveling from outside Europe.
  • Europe has an excellent public transportation system, including trains, buses, and car rentals.
  • Be aware of pickpockets and be careful in icy conditions.
  • Research local laws and customs before you travel.
