Best European Cities to Visit in December#

As a travel writer, I’ve had the privilege of exploring many European cities during my career. And let me tell you, there’s no better time to visit Europe than in December, when the festive spirit is in full swing.

The magic of Christmas fills the air, with twinkling lights, decorated trees, and enchanting markets. The smell of mulled wine and gingerbread wafts through the streets, and the sound of carols fills the air.

So, if you’re looking for a truly magical winter getaway, here are some of the best European cities to visit in December:

Prague, Czech Republic#

Prague is a city of stunning beauty, with its cobblestone streets, Gothic architecture, and winding lanes. In December, the city is transformed into a winter wonderland, with snow-covered rooftops and Christmas markets galore.

Vienna, Austria#

Vienna is known for its imperial grandeur and rich musical heritage. During the festive season, the city hosts a variety of Christmas markets, concerts, and events. And don’t miss the chance to take a ride on a horse-drawn carriage through the snow-covered streets.

Budapest, Hungary#

Budapest is a city of contrasts, with its vibrant nightlife and rich history. In December, the city comes alive with Christmas markets, ice skating rinks, and festive decorations.

Copenhagen, Denmark#

Copenhagen is a city of fairy-tale charm, with its colorful buildings, winding canals, and Tivoli Gardens amusement park. In December, the city is transformed into a winter wonderland, with Christmas markets, ice skating rinks, and festive decorations galore.

Rovaniemi, Finland#

Rovaniemi is the official hometown of Santa Claus and the perfect place to experience a truly magical Christmas. In December, the city is covered in snow, and the air is filled with the sound of sleigh bells. You can visit Santa’s Village, go on a reindeer sleigh ride, or take a snowmobile safari.

No matter which city you choose to visit, you’re sure to have a magical time in Europe in December. So, pack your bags, grab your loved ones, and get ready for an unforgettable winter getaway! Discover Hidden Gems: Lesser-Known Cities with Enchanting Winter Celebrations#

Picture yourself in a winter wonderland, where cobblestone streets are blanketed in snow, Christmas markets overflow with festive cheer, and the aroma of mulled wine fills the air. Embark on a journey to lesser-known European cities that offer a unique blend of history, culture, and holiday magic. Let’s uncover some hidden gems that will make your December travels truly unforgettable.

Riga, Latvia: A Baltic Beauty in Winter Regalia#

Stroll through the captivating streets of Riga, Latvia, a city where the past and present harmoniously coexist. Marvel at the medieval architecture of the Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, as it glistens under a dusting of snow. Discover the enchanting Christmas market in the heart of the city, where you can indulge in traditional Latvian delicacies, handcrafted gifts, and heartwarming mulled wine. Don’t miss a visit to the Riga Cathedral, whose towering spires pierce the winter sky, and the stunning Art Nouveau buildings that add a touch of elegance to the cityscape.

Sibiu, Romania: A Place of Timeless Enchantment#

Nestled in the heart of Transylvania, Sibiu, Romania, exudes a charm that transports you to a bygone era. Step into the picturesque squares, adorned with colorful buildings and twinkling lights, and feel the magic of the season come alive. Explore the historic Brukenthal Palace, which houses a remarkable art collection, and wander through the streets, discovering hidden courtyards and charming cafes. Sibiu’s Christmas market is a true delight, where you can find unique gifts, savor Romanian specialties, and experience the warmth of local hospitality.

Ghent, Belgium: A Festive Tapestry of History and Culture#

Discover the hidden gem of Ghent, Belgium, where history and culture intertwine, creating a captivating winter wonderland. Explore the medieval castle of Gravensteen, where you can delve into the city’s rich past. Admire the stunning architecture of the Ghent Altarpiece, a masterpiece of Flemish art, and stroll along the picturesque canals, adorned with festive lights. Ghent’s Christmas market, located in the heart of the city, offers a delightful array of local crafts, delectable treats, and cheerful music that will surely get you into the holiday spirit.

Copenhagen, Denmark: A Winter Wonderland of Hygge#

Experience the magic of Copenhagen, Denmark, during December, where the concept of “hygge” (pronounced hoo-gah) reigns supreme. Embrace the cozy atmosphere of the city as you walk through the snow-covered streets, admiring the colorful houses and charming shops. Visit the Tivoli Gardens, a beloved amusement park transformed into a winter wonderland, complete with twinkling lights, festive decorations, and a unique Christmas market. Don’t forget to indulge in traditional Danish pastries, such as “æbleskiver” (apple fritters) and “gløgg” (mulled wine), to truly embrace the spirit of hygge.

Colmar, France: A Storybook Village Come to Life#

Colmar, France, is a picturesque town that will make you feel like you’ve stepped into a winter fairytale. Explore the enchanting streets, lined with half-timbered houses and decorated with twinkling lights. Visit the exquisite Gothic cathedral, admire the colorful murals that adorn the buildings, and discover hidden courtyards filled with festive cheer. Colmar’s Christmas market, held in the heart of the Old Town, is a delight for the senses, offering an array of local specialties, handcrafted gifts, and heartwarming mulled wine.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Discover lesser-known European cities that offer enchanting winter celebrations, steeped in history, culture, and festive traditions.

  • Explore hidden gems like Riga, Latvia; Sibiu, Romania; Ghent, Belgium; Copenhagen, Denmark; and Colmar, France, to experience unique holiday experiences.

  • Immerse yourself in the festive atmosphere by visiting Christmas markets, admiring stunning architecture, indulging in local delicacies, and embracing the warmth of local hospitality.

  • Uncover the magic of winter in these charming cities, where the streets are adorned with twinkling lights, the air is filled with festive cheer, and the spirit of the season comes alive.

Embrace the Festive Spirit: Local Customs and Must-Try Delicacies for a Memorable Trip#

1. Prague, Czech Republic: Experience a Winter Fairy Tale#

Explore the enchanting streets of Prague, adorned with twinkling lights and festive decorations. Visit the famous Christmas markets, indulge in traditional treats like trdelník and svařák, and soak in the magic of the illuminated Old Town Square.

2. Salzburg, Austria: Immerse in Mozart’s Winter Wonderland#

Walk through the snow-covered streets of Salzburg, where you can see the birthplace of Mozart. Attend the traditional Christmas concerts, visit the Christkindlmarkt, and try local delicacies like Salzburger Nockerl and Glühwein.

3. Brussels, Belgium: Indulge in Chocolate Delights During a Winter Escape#

Discover the heart of European chocolate in Brussels. Explore the Grand Place adorned with festive decorations, visit the Christmas markets, and treat yourself to Belgian waffles, hot chocolate, and mouthwatering pralines.

4. Edinburgh, Scotland: Experience Hogmanay, a New Year’s Eve Extravaganza#

Ring in the New Year in Edinburgh with the legendary Hogmanay celebrations. Enjoy the torchlight procession, fireworks, street parties, and iconic ceilidhs. Don’t miss the chance to try traditional Scottish dishes like haggis, neeps, and tatties.

5. Budapest, Hungary: Embrace Thermal Baths and Festive Markets#

Explore the thermal baths of Budapest, renowned for their healing properties, while the city is adorned with festive lights. Visit the Christmas markets, try Chimney cakes and mulled wine, and admire the illuminated streets and bridges.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Prague: An enchanting winter fairy tale with Christmas markets, trdelník, svařák, and the magic of the Old Town Square.
  • Salzburg: Mozart’s winter wonderland with Christmas concerts, Christkindlmarkt, Salzburger Nockerl, and Glühwein.
  • Brussels: Indulge in chocolate delights at the Grand Place, Christmas markets, Belgian waffles, hot chocolate, and pralines.
  • Edinburgh: Experience Hogmanay, a grand New Year’s Eve celebration, torchlight procession, fireworks, ceilidhs, haggis, neeps, and tatties.
  • Budapest: Embrace thermal baths, Christmas markets, Chimney cakes, mulled wine, and illuminated streets and bridges.

Plan Your Winter Adventure: Planning Tips and Essential Information for a Smooth Journey#

Take a magical trip to Europe in December and immerse yourself in the charm, culture, and festive spirit of its cities. From the snow-covered streets of Vienna to the twinkling lights of Paris, each destination offers its unique enchantment. Let’s explore some must-visit European cities that will make your winter getaway unforgettable!

Experience the Magic of Vienna#

Vienna, the imperial city, welcomes you to a world of opulent palaces, classical music, and cozy coffee houses. December transforms Vienna into a winter wonderland, with the famous Christmas markets offering handmade gifts, sweet treats, and mulled wine. Attend the New Year’s Concert or visit the ice-skating rink at Rathausplatz for a memorable experience.

Stroll through the Romantic Streets of Paris#

Paris, the city of love, takes on a special aura in December. Admire the sparkling lights of the Champs-Élysées, marvel at the giant Christmas tree at Place Vendôme, or take a romantic stroll along the Seine River. Don’t miss the festive atmosphere of the Galeries Lafayette department store and the famous Christmas markets.

Discover the Festive Charm of Prague#

Prague, the city of a hundred spires, invites you to a magical winter experience. Explore the Old Town Square with its enchanting Christmas market, filled with wooden stalls selling traditional Czech crafts and delicacies. Marvel at the beauty of the Charles Bridge covered in snow and take a romantic carriage ride through the charming streets.

Embrace the Festivities in London#

London, the vibrant capital, promises a joyful December experience. Admire the stunning Christmas lights illuminating the streets, visit the iconic Winter Wonderland in Hyde Park, or go ice-skating at Somerset House. Don’t forget to indulge in a cup of warm mulled wine and a mince pie, and take in the festive atmosphere of Trafalgar Square and Covent Garden.

Unveil the Hidden Gems of Budapest#

Budapest, the city on the Danube, offers a unique blend of history, culture, and thermal baths. Discover the charming Christmas markets along Vörösmarty Square and enjoy a warm soak in one of the city’s many thermal baths. Take a stroll along the Danube River and marvel at the festive lights illuminating the city’s bridges.

Experience the Cozy Winter Charm of Bruges#

Bruges, the medieval city in Belgium, invites you to a cozy winter getaway. Wander through the cobbled streets adorned with festive decorations, visit the enchanting Christmas market at the Grote Markt, and enjoy a horse-drawn carriage ride. Take a canal boat tour to admire the city’s beauty from a different perspective, and indulge in the delicious Belgian chocolates.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Discover the enchanting atmosphere of European cities adorned with festive decorations and twinkling lights.
  • Explore the charming Christmas markets, indulge in local delicacies, and shop for unique gifts.
  • Attend festive events, concerts, and shows to immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Take romantic strolls through the snow-covered streets and marvel at the architectural wonders.
  • Experience the unique charm of each city, from the grandeur of Vienna to the romantic ambiance of Paris.
