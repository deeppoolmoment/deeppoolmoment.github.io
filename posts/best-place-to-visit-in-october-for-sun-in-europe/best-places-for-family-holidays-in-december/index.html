Best Places for Family Holidays in December#

December is a magical time for family holidays. The snow-covered landscapes, twinkling lights, and festive markets set the perfect backdrop for creating unforgettable memories. Here are some of the best places around the world that offer unique experiences and kid-friendly adventures during this special time:

1. Lapland, Finland#

Home to Santa Claus, Lapland is a winter wonderland that offers a true Arctic experience. Children can meet the big man himself, ride reindeer sleighs, and even take a swim in the icy waters of the Arctic Ocean.

2. Whistler, Canada#

Whistler is a world-class ski resort known for its beautiful slopes, lively après-ski scene, and family-friendly activities. Off the slopes, enjoy ice skating, snowshoeing, and sleigh rides.

3. Park City, Utah, USA#

Park City is a charming mountain town with a rich history and plenty of family-friendly activities. Visit the historic Main Street, take a sleigh ride through the snowy forests, or enjoy the many ski resorts in the area.

4. Queenstown, New Zealand#

Queenstown is a beautiful town nestled on the shores of Lake Wakatipu. Known as the adventure capital of New Zealand, Queenstown offers thrilling activities like bungee jumping, skydiving, and jet boating. It also has plenty of family-friendly attractions, such as the Skyline Gondola, the Kiwi Birdlife Park, and the Shotover River Jet.

5. Great Barrier Reef, Australia#

The Great Barrier Reef is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the seven natural wonders of the world. Take a boat tour to see the incredible marine life, including colorful coral, tropical fish, and sea turtles.

6. Copenhagen, Denmark#

Copenhagen is a vibrant city with a strong emphasis on family-friendly activities. Visit the Tivoli Gardens, the oldest amusement park in the world, or take a boat tour of the city’s canals.

7. Bruges, Belgium#

Bruges is a charming medieval city with a magical atmosphere, perfect for a family holiday. Visit the chocolate museum, take a boat ride on the canals, or explore the many museums and historical sites.

8. Prague, Czech Republic#

Prague is a beautiful city with a rich history and culture. Visit the Prague Castle, the Charles Bridge, and the Old Town Square. Be sure to try the delicious Czech food!

9. Vienna, Austria#

Vienna is a cultural powerhouse known for its music, art, and architecture. Visit the Schönbrunn Palace, the Vienna State Opera, and the Hofburg Palace.

10. Rovaniemi, Finland#

Rovaniemi is the capital of Lapland and the official hometown of Santa Claus. Visit the Santa Claus Village, the Arctic SnowHotel, and the Ranua Wildlife Park.

Planning Tips: Offer practical advice on flight bookings, accommodation options, packing essentials, and any necessary travel preparations.#

When it comes to family travel, the planning process can be overwhelming. But with a little preparation, you can ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip for everyone involved.

Flight Bookings:

  • Consider off-peak times: Flying during off-peak hours (e.g., early morning or late at night) can often lead to lower fares.
  • Book in advance: Book your flights as early as possible to secure the best prices and widest selection of flight times. Consider subscribing to airline email alerts for updates on sales and discounts.

Accommodation Options:

  • Choose family-friendly hotels or resorts: These accommodations often offer amenities such as kids’ clubs, pools, and babysitting services. Be sure to conduct thorough research and look for places that align with your family’s preferences and budget.
  • Consider vacation rentals: Vacation rentals provide more space and privacy, making them ideal for families with older children. These options offer a home-like setting with amenities like kitchens and laundry facilities. Be sure to choose a place in a safe neighborhood with access to essential services.

Packing Essentials:

  • Pack layers: The weather can be unpredictable, so packing layers will ensure you’re prepared for any temperature changes.
  • Comfortable shoes: As a family, you’ll likely be doing a lot of walking, so it’s essential to pack comfortable and supportive shoes. Consider bringing multiple pairs for different activities.
  • Essential documents: Keep important documents like passports, visas, and travel insurance information organized and easily accessible. Make copies of important documents and store them separately.

Necessary Travel Preparations:

  • Plan your itinerary: Before you leave, create a rough itinerary of your planned activities. This will help you make the most of your time and avoid any last-minute surprises.
  • Consider travel insurance: Travel insurance can provide peace of mind and cover unexpected events such as lost luggage, medical emergencies, or flight delays.
  • Be prepared for emergencies: Pack a first-aid kit with essential medications and supplies. It’s also wise to learn basic first aid skills before your trip.
  • Learn about the local culture: Familiarize yourself with the local customs, traditions, and laws. Respecting the local culture will enhance your travel experience and create a positive impression.

Key Takeaways:

  • Booking flights during off-peak times and in advance can lead to better deals.
  • Family-friendly hotels and vacation rentals are great accommodation options.
  • Pack layers, comfortable shoes, and essential documents.
  • Create an itinerary, consider travel insurance, be prepared for emergencies, and learn about the local culture.

FAQ#

Q1: What are some must-visit destinations for family holidays in December?#

A1: For a magical winter wonderland, consider destinations like Lapland in Finland, where families can experience sleigh rides, husky safaris, and meet Santa Claus himself. For sun-seekers, tropical paradises like the Maldives, Mauritius, and the Caribbean offer warm weather, pristine beaches, and countless family-friendly activities. For a mix of culture, history, and festive cheer, European cities like London, Paris, and Rome transform into enchanting winter wonderlands, adorned with festive decorations and offering unique holiday experiences.

Q2: How can families plan an itinerary that caters to different interests and ages?#

A2: When planning an itinerary, consider the interests and preferences of all family members. For adventure-loving families, incorporate activities like skiing, snowboarding, or exploring natural wonders. For those interested in culture and history, visit museums, historical sites, or attend cultural events. For families with younger children, prioritize destinations with amusement parks, water parks, or interactive attractions. By striking a balance between different interests, families can create a memorable and enjoyable holiday experience for everyone.

Q3: What are some tips for ensuring a smooth and stress-free family holiday in December?#

A3: To ensure a smooth holiday experience, start planning well in advance, especially when traveling during peak season. Book flights, accommodations, and activities early to avoid last-minute surprises. Consider purchasing travel insurance to protect your family against unforeseen circumstances. Pack accordingly, ensuring you have warm clothes, essentials, and entertainment for the journey. Research local customs and traditions to avoid cultural misunderstandings. Finally, remember to prioritize flexibility and be prepared to adapt to unexpected changes or weather conditions.

Q4: How can families save money while planning a family holiday in December?#

A4: To save money while planning a family holiday in December, consider traveling during the shoulder season (early or late December) instead of the peak holiday period. Look for destinations offering discounts or family-friendly packages. Take advantage of loyalty programs or travel rewards to accumulate points or discounts. Consider self-catering options or eating at local restaurants to save on dining expenses. Pack light to avoid excess baggage fees. By planning wisely and being flexible, families can enjoy a wonderful holiday experience without breaking the bank.

Q5: What are some activities that families can enjoy together during a December holiday?#

A5: December offers a plethora of family-friendly activities. From building snowmen and having snowball fights in snowy destinations to swimming, snorkeling, and enjoying water sports in tropical locations, families can create unforgettable memories. Visit festive markets, ice-skating rinks, or holiday-themed events for a truly magical experience. Take advantage of the opportunity to explore local traditions and customs, whether it’s attending a Christmas mass, celebrating Hanukkah, or learning about cultural practices in different destinations. By embracing the spirit of the season and spending quality time together, families can create cherished memories that will last a lifetime.
