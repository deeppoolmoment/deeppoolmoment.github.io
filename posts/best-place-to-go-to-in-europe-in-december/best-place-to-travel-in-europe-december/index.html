Best Place to Travel in Europe December#

Envision yourself wandering through snow-covered streets adorned with twinkling lights, or marveling at enchanting Christmas markets brimming with festive cheer. Europe in December transforms into a winter wonderland, promising unforgettable holiday experiences. Let me unveil the best places to travel in Europe in December and guide you toward a magical winter escapade.

1. Prague, Czech Republic#

Prepare to be enchanted by Prague’s Old Town Square, where a towering Christmas tree sparkles amidst a symphony of festive lights. Explore the city’s historic heart adorned with ornate decorations and indulge in traditional Czech delicacies at charming outdoor markets. Don’t miss the opportunity to witness the renowned Prague Advent Singing Festival, filling the air with joyous melodies.

2. Vienna, Austria#

Immerse yourself in the imperial grandeur of Vienna, where palaces and museums come alive with festive spirit. Stroll along the enchanting Christmas markets lining the streets, relishing the aromas of roasted chestnuts and mulled wine. Be mesmerized by the Vienna Boys’ Choir performing traditional carols in opulent concert halls.

3. Budapest, Hungary#

Discover the enchanting beauty of Budapest, adorned with twinkling lights and festive decorations. Glide along the Danube River on a boat cruise, marveling at the illuminated landmarks. Experience the warmth of traditional Hungarian hospitality at the Christmas markets and indulge in delectable local treats like kürtőskalács, a sweet chimney cake.

4. Copenhagen, Denmark#

Embrace the hygge lifestyle in Copenhagen, where coziness and contentment prevail during the festive season. Stroll through Tivoli Gardens, transformed into a winter wonderland with twinkling lights, ice skating rinks, and festive market stalls. Warm up with a cup of gløgg, a traditional Danish mulled wine, and savor the delectable ‘æbleskiver’, donut-like treats sprinkled with powdered sugar.

5. Rovaniemi, Finland#

Venture into the Arctic Circle and experience the magic of Rovaniemi, the official hometown of Santa Claus. Meet Santa and his reindeer, embark on a thrilling reindeer or husky safari, and witness the Northern Lights dancing across the winter sky. Rovaniemi is a winter wonderland straight out of a fairy tale.

6. Edinburgh, Scotland#

Discover the charm of Edinburgh during the festive season, where history and tradition blend seamlessly. Explore the city’s medieval Old Town adorned with twinkling lights, and marvel at the majestic Edinburgh Castle illuminated against the winter sky. Immerse yourself in the enchanting Christmas markets, savor traditional Scottish fare, and raise a glass of whisky to celebrate the season.

7. Bruges, Belgium#

Step back in time and wander through the cobblestone streets of Bruges, a medieval city that exudes festive charm. Admire the intricate decorations adorning the canals and bridges, and indulge in Belgian chocolate and waffles, local specialties that warm both the heart and soul.

No matter where your wanderlust takes you, Europe in December promises an enchanting and unforgettable holiday experience. Embrace the festive spirit, marvel at the winter wonders, and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Go Skiing in the Austrian Alps: The Ultimate Winter Wonderland Adventure#

As the holiday season approaches, there’s no better way to celebrate than with a magical skiing adventure in the Austrian Alps. With its pristine slopes, breathtaking scenery, and charming mountain towns, Austria offers an unforgettable winter experience for skiers of all levels. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or just starting, here’s why you should make skiing in the Austrian Alps your top travel destination this December:

Unveiling the Unparalleled Beauty of the Austrian Alps#

The Austrian Alps are a skier’s paradise, boasting some of the most stunning landscapes in Europe. Picture yourself gliding down perfectly groomed runs, surrounded by snow-capped peaks, glistening glaciers, and picturesque villages nestled in the valleys below. From the iconic slopes of Kitzbühel to the hidden gems of the Arlberg region, the Austrian Alps offer endless opportunities for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.

World-Class Ski Resorts for Every Level#

Austria is home to some of the world’s most renowned ski resorts, each offering a unique experience tailored to skiers of varying abilities. Whether you prefer challenging black diamond runs or gentle beginner slopes, you’ll find the perfect resort to suit your needs. With state-of-the-art facilities, modern lifts, and highly trained instructors, you’ll have everything you need for a safe and enjoyable skiing experience.

A Cultural and Culinary Journey Through the Austrian Alps#

Beyond the slopes, the Austrian Alps offer a rich cultural and culinary experience. Discover charming mountain villages adorned with traditional Tyrolean architecture, where you can indulge in delectable local cuisine and warm up with a glass of mulled wine after a day on the slopes. From cozy mountain huts serving hearty Austrian dishes to Michelin-starred restaurants with innovative Alpine fare, you’ll find culinary delights to satisfy every palate.

Go skiing in the Austrian Alps: The Ultimate Winter Getaway#

If you’re looking for an unforgettable winter getaway, skiing in the Austrian Alps is the perfect choice. With its stunning scenery, world-class ski resorts, and charming cultural experiences, the Austrian Alps offer a magical holiday adventure that will leave you with lasting memories. Book your trip today and experience the ultimate skiing paradise!

Key Takeaways:#

  • Experience the breathtaking beauty of the Austrian Alps while skiing down pristine slopes surrounded by majestic mountains.
  • Choose from a wide range of world-class ski resorts catering to skiers of all levels, from beginners to seasoned pros.
  • Immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of the region, exploring charming mountain villages and savoring delectable Austrian cuisine.
  • Create unforgettable holiday memories with friends and family, enjoying the perfect winter getaway in the heart of the Alps.

Celebrate the holidays in Prague, Czech Republic.#

Prague, the captivating capital of the Czech Republic, exudes an enchanting charm during the festive season, transforming into a winter wonderland adorned with twinkling lights and festive decorations. From enchanting Christmas markets to heartwarming traditions and stunning winter landscapes, Prague offers an unforgettable holiday experience.

Experience the Magic of Prague’s Christmas Markets:#

Stroll through the enchanting Christmas markets that adorn Prague’s Old Town Square and Wenceslas Square. These markets offer a treasure trove of traditional Czech crafts, hand-blown glass ornaments, delicious local treats, and mulled wine to warm your spirits.

Marvel at the City’s Stunning Architecture:#

Prague’s architectural marvels shine even brighter during the festive season. Admire the Gothic spires of Prague Castle, the iconic Charles Bridge, and the Astronomical Clock adorned with holiday decorations.

Indulge in Traditional Czech Cuisine:#

Savor the flavors of traditional Czech cuisine, including hearty goulash, roasted goose, and sweet pastries. Warm your hands with a cup of svařák, a mulled wine that adds cheer to the festive atmosphere.

Explore Prague’s Cultural Heritage:#

Immerse yourself in Prague’s rich history and culture. Visit the Old Town Hall, home to the Astronomical Clock, and explore the many museums and art galleries that showcase the city’s artistic and cultural legacy.

Embark on a Festive Walking Tour:#

Join a guided walking tour to discover Prague’s hidden gems and learn about the city’s fascinating holiday traditions. Marvel at the festive decorations and soak in the joyful atmosphere that fills the streets.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Experience the magic of Prague’s Christmas markets, adorned with twinkling lights and offering traditional crafts and festive treats.
  • Marvel at the city’s stunning architecture, including Prague Castle, Charles Bridge, and the Astronomical Clock, all adorned with holiday decorations.
  • Indulge in traditional Czech cuisine, such as hearty goulash, roasted goose, and sweet pastries, and warm up with a cup of svařák, a mulled wine.
  • Explore Prague’s cultural heritage by visiting the Old Town Hall, museums, and art galleries, and learn about the city’s fascinating holiday traditions.
  • Embark on a festive walking tour to discover hidden gems and soak in the joyful atmosphere that fills the streets.

Take a winter cruise on the Danube River.#

Imagine cruising along the majestic Danube River during winter, surrounded by snow-covered landscapes and charming towns adorned with festive lights. Experience the magic of Christmas markets, enchanting castles, and stunning natural wonders as you sail through some of Europe’s most captivating destinations.

The Enchanting Danube River Cruise#

A winter cruise on the Danube River is an extraordinary journey that promises unforgettable moments. Here are some highlights of this enchanting experience:

  • Marvel at the captivating beauty of the snow-covered landscapes along the riverbanks.

  • Immerse yourself in the festive atmosphere of Christmas markets, filled with delightful aromas, twinkling lights, and unique gifts.

  • Step ashore to explore historic towns and cities, each with its own unique charm and historical significance.

  • Visit iconic landmarks such as castles, cathedrals, and museums, where you’ll discover the rich cultural heritage of the region.

  • Indulge in delicious local cuisine and warm up with a glass of mulled wine or a steaming cup of hot chocolate.

Danube River Cruise Itineraries#

Various cruise itineraries are available, ranging from short, weekend getaways to longer, in-depth journeys. Some popular routes include:

  • Budapest to Vienna: This classic route takes you through the heart of Central Europe, showcasing the stunning capitals of Hungary and Austria.

  • Passau to Budapest: Embark on a voyage from Germany to Hungary, exploring the picturesque towns and landscapes along the way.

  • Vienna to Bratislava: Discover the vibrant cities of Vienna and Bratislava, with their rich history, charming architecture, and lively nightlife.

  • Nuremberg to Budapest: This longer itinerary allows you to delve into the history and culture of Germany, Austria, and Hungary, with stops at UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Key Takeaways:#

  • A winter cruise on the Danube River offers a unique and unforgettable holiday experience.

  • Enjoy the festive atmosphere of Christmas markets and explore charming towns adorned with decorations.

  • Visit iconic landmarks, indulge in local cuisine, and soak in the stunning scenery.

  • Choose from various cruise itineraries, ranging from short getaways to longer journeys.

  • Experience the magic of Europe’s enchanting winter wonderland from the comfort of a river cruise.
