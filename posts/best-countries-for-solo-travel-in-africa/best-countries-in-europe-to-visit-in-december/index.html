Best Countries in Europe to Visit in December#

In December, Europe transforms into a winter wonderland, beckoning travelers with its festive charm, enchanting landscapes, and unique cultural experiences. From snow-covered streets adorned with twinkling lights to bustling Christmas markets filled with sweet aromas and cheerful melodies, Europe in December offers an unforgettable escapade.

Germany#

Germany, renowned for its rich history, charming towns, and world-class Christmas markets, is a must-visit destination in December. Stroll through the streets of Berlin, Munich, or Nuremberg and be mesmerized by the festive decorations, indulge in delectable treats like gingerbread cookies and mulled wine, and immerse yourself in the joyous atmosphere of the Christmas markets.

France#

France, known for its captivating cities, romantic ambiance, and culinary delights, offers a magical experience in December. Explore the iconic landmarks of Paris adorned with holiday lights, visit the enchanting Christmas markets in Strasbourg or Colmar, and indulge in the festive delicacies like bûche de Noël (Yule log) and champagne.

Austria#

Austria, famous for its stunning Alpine landscapes, rich musical heritage, and vibrant Christmas traditions, is a winter wonderland waiting to be discovered. Visit the charming cities of Vienna or Salzburg, where you can admire the festive decorations, attend traditional concerts, and indulge in delicious treats like Salzburger Nockerl (fluffy soufflé dumplings) and Glühwein (mulled wine).

Italy#

Italy, renowned for its artistic treasures, delectable cuisine, and lively atmosphere, offers a vibrant and festive experience in December. Explore the historic streets of Rome or Florence adorned with Christmas lights, visit the picturesque Christmas markets in Verona or Bolzano, and savor the festive delicacies like panettone (sweet bread) and spumante (sparkling wine).

Switzerland#

Switzerland, known for its breathtaking mountains, pristine lakes, and luxurious resorts, offers a magical winter escape in December. Visit the charming villages of Zermatt or St. Moritz, where you can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities, explore the enchanting Christmas markets, and indulge in Swiss specialties like fondue and raclette.

Tips for Planning Your Trip to Europe in December#

  • Plan in Advance: Book your flights, accommodations, and rental cars early to secure the best deals and avoid last-minute surprises.

  • Pack Warm: December in Europe can be chilly, so pack warm clothing, including a jacket, scarf, hat, and gloves.

  • Check Visa Requirements: Make sure you have the necessary visas and travel documents to enter each country you plan to visit.

  • Learn Some Basic Phrases: Knowing a few basic phrases in the local language can go a long way in enhancing your travel experience.

  • Embrace the Festive Spirit: December is a time for celebration in Europe, so embrace the festive spirit by attending Christmas markets, enjoying local delicacies, and participating in holiday traditions.

  • Be Flexible: Things don’t always go according to plan when traveling, so be flexible and open to changes in your itinerary if necessary.

Countries with rich cultural heritage and festive traditions#

Vienna, the imperial city, invites you to waltz through its majestic streets, adorned with sparkling lights and bustling with Christmas markets. Sip on warm Gluhwein as you browse handmade ornaments and savor delectable treats. Don’t miss the chance to witness the Vienna Boys’ Choir perform enchanting carols in grand cathedrals.

Next, let’s head to the snow-covered wonderland of Finland. Lapland, the mystical home of Santa Claus, beckons you to embark on a thrilling reindeer sleigh ride, marvel at the ethereal Northern Lights, and visit the enchanting Santa Claus Village. Embrace the spirit of Christmas in the heart of the Arctic Circle, where traditions run deep and the magic of the season comes alive.

Stroll through the cobblestone streets of Nuremberg, Munich, or Dresden, and immerse yourself in the sights, sounds, and aromas of the season. Witness the spectacle of Christmas parades and concerts, and indulge in the warmth of traditional German hospitality.

Now, let’s venture to the picturesque Switzerland. Enchanting villages like Zermatt and St. Moritz transform into winter wonderlands, offering a perfect blend of festive charm and alpine adventure. Glide down pristine slopes, soak in thermal baths surrounded by snow-capped peaks, and indulge in traditional Swiss cuisine by a crackling fireplace. Embrace the tranquility of the Swiss Alps and let the festive spirit wash over you.

Tallinn, the medieval capital, captivates with its fairy-tale-like Old Town, adorned with twinkling lights and enchanting Christmas decorations. Embrace the Estonian spirit of Joul by joining in traditional dances and songs, making your December adventure truly unforgettable.

Hidden gems with off-the-beaten-path experiences#

Embrace the Mystique: Discovering Unique European Destinations in December

December beckons travelers with its enchanting charm, painting Europe in a picturesque tapestry of snowy landscapes, twinkling lights, and festive cheer. As you traverse this magical continent, seek out hidden gems with off-the-beaten-path experiences that promise unforgettable moments.

Unveiling the Enchantment of Europe’s Secret Destinations#

  1. Norway’s Arctic Adventure:

    Venture beyond the conventional tourist trails and embark on a thrilling adventure in Norway’s Arctic wilderness. Witness the captivating Northern Lights dancing across the starlit sky, immerse yourself in Sami culture, and partake in exhilarating activities like dog sledding and snowshoeing.

  2. Iceland’s Winter Wonderland:

    Iceland’s mesmerizing beauty transcends words, especially in December. Discover the ethereal beauty of frozen waterfalls, explore ice caves hidden beneath glaciers, and chase the elusive Northern Lights. Adventure awaits at every turn, from relaxing in geothermal hot springs to embarking on glacier hikes.

  3. Romania’s Enchanting Villages:

    Discover the unspoiled charm of Romania’s countryside, where quaint villages and medieval towns whisper tales of old. Explore the painted churches of Bucovina, marvel at the grandeur of Peleş Castle, and delve into the fascinating history and culture of this captivating region.

  4. Finland’s Arctic Retreat:

    Escape the hustle and bustle in Finland’s serene Arctic haven. Experience the tranquility of snow-covered forests, where silence is broken only by the crunch of snow beneath your boots. Enjoy traditional Finnish saunas, indulge in reindeer-sledding excursions, and soak in the awe-inspiring beauty of the Arctic Circle.

  5. Scotland’s Highland Hideaways:

    Unveil the mystique of Scotland’s Highlands, where rugged mountains, misty glens, and ancient castles paint a captivating picture. Walk in the footsteps of legends as you explore iconic sites like Loch Ness and Eilean Donan Castle. Immerse yourself in the warmth of Scottish hospitality and embrace the region’s rich history and folklore.

Practical travel tips and considerations for planning a December trip to Europe#

Whether you dream of strolling through snow-covered streets lined with twinkling lights or indulging in festive delicacies, Europe in December is a captivating destination that offers an abundance of enchanting experiences. To make the most of your winter getaway, keep these essential tips in mind:

Plan Early:#

  • December is a popular time to visit Europe, so booking your flights, accommodation, and tours well in advance is essential. Early planning ensures availability and helps you secure the best deals.
  • Pack Wisely:
  • Embrace the cold with warm clothing, including a thick coat, gloves, and a hat. Pack waterproof gear to combat rain or snow. Remember comfortable walking shoes for exploring cities and towns steeped in history.
  • Experience Festive Markets:
  • Dive into the heart of European Christmas spirit by visiting festive markets. Find unique gifts, savor local treats, and soak in the enchanting ambience.
  • Prioritize Walkability:
  • Many European cities are best explored on foot. Choose accommodations in central locations to minimize time spent on transportation. You’ll discover hidden gems and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere.
  • Plan Your Itinerary:
  • Research attractions, museums, and events you wish to experience. Create a realistic itinerary that allows for flexibility and spontaneity. Consider visiting smaller towns and villages for a more authentic European experience.
  • Embrace Outdoor Activities:
  • Embrace the winter wonderland with outdoor activities like skiing, snowboarding, snowshoeing, or ice skating. These pursuits provide exhilarating ways to enjoy the snowy landscapes.
  • Check Transportation Options:
  • Research local transportation options, including trains, buses, and rental cars. Public transportation is often reliable and affordable. Rental cars offer more flexibility but may require navigating unfamiliar roads and parking challenges.
  • Respect Local Customs and Traditions:
  • Familiarize yourself with local customs and traditions to ensure respectful interactions with locals. Learn basic phrases in the local language to enhance your communication and cultural understanding.
  • Enjoy Local Cuisine:
  • Indulge in the culinary delights of each region. Try traditional dishes, warm beverages, and festive treats. Many European cities offer food tours that provide insights into the local cuisine.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Plan early for flights, accommodation, and tours to secure availability and deals.
  • Pack wisely with warm clothing, waterproof gear, and comfortable walking shoes.
  • Experience the magic of festive markets, offering unique gifts and delightful treats.
  • Prioritize walkability by choosing centrally located accommodations and exploring on foot.
  • Create a realistic itinerary that combines popular attractions with hidden gems.
  • Embrace outdoor activities like skiing, snowboarding, snowshoeing, or ice skating.
  • Research transportation options, considering trains, buses, and rental cars.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, learning basic phrases for enhanced communication.
  • Indulge in the local cuisine, trying traditional dishes, warm beverages, and festive treats.

FAQ#

Q1: Why is December a great time to visit Europe?#

A1: December offers travelers the chance to experience the magical charm of European cities and towns during the festive season, with enchanting Christmas markets, festive illuminations, and unique cultural traditions.

Q2: Which European countries offer the best Christmas markets?#

A2: Some of the most renowned Christmas markets in Europe can be found in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and France. These markets feature an array of traditional crafts, local delicacies, and festive decorations.

Q3: Are there any specific events or festivals that take place in Europe during December?#

A3: Yes, December is a month filled with festive events and celebrations across Europe. Some notable examples include the Christmas markets in Germany and Austria, the New Year’s Eve celebrations in Paris and London, and the Winter Festival in Bruges.

Q4: What are some popular outdoor activities to enjoy in Europe during December?#

A4: Travelers can indulge in a variety of outdoor activities during December in Europe, including ice skating in picturesque city centers, skiing and snowboarding in the stunning Alpine regions, and exploring the charming Christmas markets nestled in historic squares.

Q5: Are there any tips for planning a winter trip to Europe in December?#

A5: To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip, it’s advisable to plan ahead by booking accommodations and transportation in advance, packing warm clothing and appropriate footwear, and researching local customs and traditions to make the most of the festive atmosphere.
