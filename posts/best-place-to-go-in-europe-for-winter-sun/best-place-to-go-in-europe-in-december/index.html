Best place to go in Europe in December: Discover Enchanting Winter Delights#

The best place to go in Europe in December is a question every traveler asks themselves when seeking a magical winter escape. Europe transforms into a winter wonderland in December, with snow-covered landscapes, twinkling Christmas lights, and festive cheer filling the air.

H3: Explore the Heart of Festive Traditions: Strasbourg, France

Strasbourg, France, oozes with festive charm. Stroll through its enchanting Christmas market, the oldest in France, and admire the intricate decorations adorning the half-timbered houses. Don’t miss the chance to taste the traditional “bredele” cookies and sip on mulled wine as you soak up the festive ambiance.

H3: Experience the Winter Magic of Vienna, Austria

Vienna, Austria, is a feast for the senses during December. Indulge in a horse-drawn carriage ride through the snow-covered streets, marvel at the festive lights illuminating the city, and attend a classical concert at the Musikverein. Don’t forget to visit the Schönbrunn Palace and explore its stunning Christmas market.

H3: Discover a Sparkling Winter Wonderland in Copenhagen, Denmark

Copenhagen, Denmark, exudes a cozy and charming atmosphere during the winter season. Stroll through the Tivoli Gardens amusement park, transformed into a winter wonderland with twinkling lights, ice-skating rinks, and festive decorations. Take a boat tour along the picturesque canals to experience the city from a different perspective.

H3: Immerse Yourself in the Festive Spirit of Prague, Czech Republic

Prague, Czech Republic, is a captivating destination during December. Wander through the Old Town Square, adorned with a majestic Christmas tree and surrounded by vibrant Christmas markets. Explore the city’s medieval streets, lined with charming shops and restaurants, and enjoy a traditional Czech dinner accompanied by mulled wine.

H3: Embark on a Winter Adventure in Lapland, Finland

Lapland, Finland, is a dream destination for those seeking a truly magical winter experience. Stay in a cozy log cabin, go on a husky safari, and witness the awe-inspiring Northern Lights dancing across the sky. Embark on a reindeer sleigh ride through the snowy forests and indulge in a traditional Finnish sauna to complete your Arctic adventure.

Prague: A Festive Wonderland#

  • Marvel at the beauty of the Old Town Square Christmas Market. Immerse yourself in the festive spirit as you stroll through this bustling square, adorned with twinkling lights and festive decorations. Browse the stalls selling traditional Czech handicrafts, savor delicious local treats, and sip on mulled wine to warm up on a cold winter’s day.

  • Explore the city’s rich history and culture at the Prague Castle. Discover the grandeur of this UNESCO World Heritage Site, which houses some of Prague’s most iconic landmarks, including St. Vitus Cathedral and the Golden Lane.

  • Take a romantic horse-drawn carriage ride through the snow-covered streets. Cuddle up under a blanket and admire the city’s stunning architecture as you glide through its charming streets.

  • Enjoy a traditional Czech Christmas dinner at one of the city’s many restaurants. Indulge in hearty dishes like roast goose, potato dumplings, and red cabbage, accompanied by a glass of Czech beer or wine.

Paris: A City of Enchantment#

  • Stroll along the Champs-Élysées, adorned with festive lights and decorations. Experience the magic of Paris during the holiday season as you admire the dazzling displays of lights and decorations along this iconic boulevard.

  • Visit the Christmas market at the Tuileries Garden. This charming market offers a variety of festive treats and gifts, as well as a chance to admire the beautiful views of the Louvre Museum and the Eiffel Tower.

  • Take a leisurely cruise along the Seine River. Glide past some of Paris’s most famous landmarks, including the Notre Dame Cathedral and the Eiffel Tower, as you enjoy a glass of champagne and soak in the festive atmosphere.

  • Indulge in a delicious French Christmas dinner at a traditional bistro. Savor delicacies like foie gras, escargot, and bûche de Noël, all paired with a bottle of fine French wine.

London: A Festive Extravaganza#

  • Visit the iconic Winter Wonderland in Hyde Park. This festive extravaganza features a variety of attractions, including an ice rink, a Christmas market, and a circus. You can also enjoy live music, shows, and festive treats.

  • Take a walk through the magical Christmas lights at Kew Gardens. Immerse yourself in a world of twinkling lights and festive decorations as you explore this beautiful botanical garden.

  • See a performance of The Nutcracker at the Royal Opera House. Experience the magic of this classic Christmas ballet, performed by the world-renowned Royal Opera House dancers.

  • Enjoy a traditional British Christmas dinner at a cozy pub. Indulge in a hearty meal of roast turkey, stuffing, and gravy, followed by a slice of Christmas pudding with brandy sauce.

FAQ#

Q1: Which European cities offer the most enchanting winter experiences in December?#

A1: Cities like Salzburg, Vienna, Prague, and Rovaniemi offer magical winter experiences during December. With festive markets, charming streets adorned with lights, and an array of cultural events, these destinations promise an unforgettable holiday atmosphere.

Q2: Are there any hidden gems in Europe that are particularly stunning during the winter season?#

A2: Absolutely! Consider exploring lesser-known destinations like Hallstatt in Austria, Bled in Slovenia, and Colmar in France. These charming towns transform into winter wonderlands, offering picturesque scenery, cozy accommodations, and unique traditions.

Q3: What are some of the must-visit attractions in Europe during December?#

A3: Don’t miss iconic attractions such as the Christmas markets in Strasbourg, the ice skating rink at Rockefeller Center in New York City, and the festive illuminations along London’s Oxford Street. These attractions capture the essence of the holiday spirit and offer unforgettable experiences.

Q4: Can you recommend any unique winter activities or events in Europe?#

A4: Embrace the festive spirit by attending traditional Christmas concerts, ice sculpting competitions, and winter festivals. Consider activities like snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, and sleigh rides to fully immerse yourself in the magic of the season.

Q5: What are some tips for planning a winter trip to Europe in December?#

A5: Plan your itinerary carefully to make the most of the festive atmosphere. Book accommodations and transportation in advance to avoid last-minute surprises. Pack warm clothing, including waterproof gear, and check weather forecasts to prepare for varying conditions.
