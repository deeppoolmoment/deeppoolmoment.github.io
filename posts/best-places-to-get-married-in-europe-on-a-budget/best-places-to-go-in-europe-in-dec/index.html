Best Places to Go in Europe in Dec#

Ahh, December in Europe! With the festive decorations, cozy atmosphere, and enchanting holiday traditions, Europe transforms into a magical wonderland during this time of year. As a passionate travel blogger with years of experience exploring Europe’s hidden gems, I’ve compiled a list of the best places to visit in December that will capture your heart and make your holiday season truly unforgettable.

1. Prague, Czech Republic#

Prague, the capital of the Czech Republic and home to stunning Gothic architecture, is a must-visit destination in December. Stroll through the charming Old Town Square, where you’ll find a Christmas market filled with delightful treats and unique gifts. Don’t miss the opportunity to visit Prague Castle and witness the magical views of the city from its courtyards. And of course, indulge in the delicious traditional Czech cuisine, including hearty goulash and sweet trdelník.

2. Lapland, Finland#

If you dream of experiencing a true winter wonderland, head to Lapland, Finland, where you can witness the Northern Lights dancing across the sky. Take a reindeer or husky-pulled sleigh ride through the snowy forests, visit the Santa Claus Village, and enjoy plenty of outdoor activities like snowshoeing and cross-country skiing.

3. Bruges, Belgium#

Bruges, with its cobblestone streets, picturesque canals, and medieval architecture, is a charming city that becomes even more magical during the Christmas season. Visit the Bruges Christmas Market, where you can find handmade ornaments, local delicacies, and indulge in warm mulled wine. Take a romantic boat tour through the canals, admire the stunning light displays, and savor the delicious Belgian chocolates.

4. Vienna, Austria#

Vienna, known for its grand palaces, elegant coffee houses, and rich musical heritage, transforms into a winter wonderland in December. Visit the Schönbrunn Palace Christmas Market, where you can enjoy traditional Austrian dishes, festive decorations, and live music. Take a stroll through the Belvedere Palace gardens, where you’ll find ice skating rinks and stunning views of the city. And don’t forget to indulge in Viennese pastries like sachertorte and apple strudel.

5. Budapest, Hungary#

Budapest, with its thermal baths, stunning architecture, and vibrant nightlife, is a captivating destination any time of year. In December, the city takes on a special charm with Christmas markets, festive lights, and concerts. Visit the Budapest Christmas Fair at Vörösmarty Square, where you can find unique gifts, traditional Hungarian food, and plenty of mulled wine. Take a boat tour on the Danube River to admire the illuminated bridges and buildings, and enjoy the lively atmosphere of the city’s ruin bars.

No matter which destination you choose, Europe in December offers a unique and unforgettable holiday experience. Embrace the festive spirit, indulge in local traditions, and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Festive Celebrations and Traditions#

Whether you seek bustling Christmas markets or serene winter wonderlands, Europe offers a captivating array of festive experiences in December. From enchanting medieval towns adorned with twinkling lights to grand cities pulsating with holiday cheer, each destination promises a unique blend of merriment and charm.

Strolling Through a Winter Wonderland#

  • Embrace the magic of Vienna’s Christmas markets: Explore the enchanting stalls selling traditional crafts, indulge in delectable treats like gingerbread cookies and mulled wine, and take in the captivating ambiance of the city’s grand squares.

  • Immerse yourself in the medieval charm of Prague: Wander through the cobbled streets of the Old Town, marvel at the stunning Astronomical Clock, and soak in the festive atmosphere of the annual Christmas markets.

  • Discover the festive magic of Budapest: Admire the city’s iconic landmarks illuminated for the holidays, take a romantic horse-drawn carriage ride through the snow-covered streets, and enjoy the vibrant Christmas markets.

  • Experience the serene beauty of Lapland: Venture to the Arctic Circle and be mesmerized by the ethereal Northern Lights, engage in thrilling winter activities like reindeer sleigh rides and snowmobiling, and immerse yourself in the mystical Sami culture.

  • Embrace the festive spirit of London: Explore the renowned winter wonderland of Hyde Park, marvel at the dazzling decorations along Oxford Street, and experience the vibrant atmosphere of the city’s many Christmas markets.

Splendid Festive Celebrations and Traditions#

  • Witness the grandeur of Rome’s Christmas celebrations: Admire the awe-inspiring nativity scenes, attend the traditional Midnight Mass at the Vatican, and indulge in the delectable treats of the city’s Christmas markets.

  • Immerse yourself in the festive charm of Paris: Stroll along the Champs-Élysées adorned with twinkling lights, visit the enchanting Christmas markets, and witness the spectacular Eiffel Tower light show.

  • Discover the festive traditions of Stockholm: Explore the city’s charming Christmas markets, indulge in traditional Swedish delicacies like glögg and gingerbread cookies, and immerse yourself in the city’s beautiful winter wonderland.

  • Experience the festive delights of Copenhagen: Admire the city’s iconic Tivoli Gardens transformed into a winter wonderland, visit the traditional Christmas markets, and take a romantic boat ride along the picturesque canals.

  • Celebrate the festive season in Berlin: Explore the city’s many Christmas markets, marvel at the stunning decorations along Kurfürstendamm, and experience the vibrant atmosphere of the city’s festive celebrations.

  • Enchanting Destinations: From medieval towns to grand cities, Europe’s diverse destinations provide a variety of festive experiences.

  • Festive Traditions: Embrace the unique traditions of each destination, from attending traditional Midnight Mass in Rome to indulging in Swedish glögg.

Unforgettable Day Trips and Excursions#

Have you ever imagined spending the festive month of December surrounded by centuries-old castles, snow-covered landscapes, and bustling Christmas markets? Europe, with its rich history, vibrant culture, and picturesque beauty, offers an array of destinations that will turn your December holiday into an unforgettable memory. Get ready to be enchanted as we unveil the hidden gems and top attractions that await you during this magical time of the year.

Discover the Authentic Charm of Salzburg, Austria#

Nestled amidst the snow-capped Austrian Alps, Salzburg is a city that effortlessly blends history and festivity. Explore the birthplace of Mozart, stroll through the Getreidegasse, a charming cobblestone street lined with festive decorations, and soak in the sights of the Hohensalzburg Fortress. Don’t miss the opportunity to indulge in traditional Austrian delicacies like Salzburger Nockerl, a fluffy soufflé-like dessert.

Marvel at the Winter Wonderland of Prague, Czech Republic#

Prague, the ‘City of a Hundred Spires’, transforms into a fairytale wonderland during December. Wander through the Old Town Square, where the Christmas market envelops the air with the scent of roasted chestnuts and mulled wine. Witness the grandeur of the Charles Bridge, adorned with twinkling lights, and take a leisurely stroll along the Vltava River. Embrace the festive spirit by enjoying a traditional Czech Christmas dinner, featuring roast goose with dumplings and red cabbage.

Explore the Festive Delights of Colmar, France#

Travel to Colmar, a picturesque town in the heart of Alsace, and experience a truly magical Alsatian Christmas. Step into the enchanting old town, where half-timbered houses adorned with festive lights create a storybook-like atmosphere. Explore the charming Christmas markets, offering an array of local delicacies, handcrafted ornaments, and mulled wine. Don’t miss the opportunity to visit the Colmar Christmas Market, renowned for its giant Christmas tree and festive ambiance.

Discover the Enchanting Beauty of Bruges, Belgium#

Immerse yourself in the medieval splendor of Bruges, where canals lined with colorful buildings create a picturesque backdrop for your December journey. Admire the stunning architecture of the Market Square, illuminated by twinkling lights, and glide along the frozen canals on a romantic boat tour. Indulge in the delectable Belgian chocolates and waffles, and experience the heartwarming tradition of ‘Secret Santa’ by exchanging gifts with loved ones.

Experience the Festive Spirit of Budapest, Hungary#

Budapest, the ‘Pearl of the Danube’, sparkles with festive charm during December. Explore the Christmas markets scattered throughout the city, offering a delightful mix of traditional Hungarian crafts, local delicacies, and mulled wine. Take a leisurely stroll along the Danube River, admiring the illuminated bridges and the grandeur of the Hungarian Parliament Building. Warm up with a steaming bowl of goulash, a traditional Hungarian stew, and embrace the joyful spirit of the season.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Salzburg, Austria: Explore the birthplace of Mozart and indulge in traditional Austrian delicacies.
  • Prague, Czech Republic: Witness the grandeur of the Charles Bridge and enjoy traditional Czech Christmas dinner.
  • Colmar, France: Discover the enchanting Alsatian Christmas and visit the Colmar Christmas Market.
  • Bruges, Belgium: Admire the medieval splendor and indulge in Belgian chocolates and waffles.
  • Budapest, Hungary: Explore the Christmas markets and experience the heartwarming tradition of ‘Secret Santa’.

Local Delicacies and Seasonal Menus#

In Europe, December is a time of spirited festivals, cozy markets, twinkling lights, and an abundance of local delicacies and seasonal menus that reflect the festive spirit.

Here’s a culinary journey through some European destinations known for their unique flavors:

Salzburg, Austria:#

  • Indulge in “Schnitzel,” a crispy breaded cutlet, often served with potato salad and lingonberry jam.
  • Try “Glühwein,” a warm mulled wine infused with spices like cinnamon and clove.
  • Satisfy your sweet tooth with “Salzburger Nockerl,” a fluffy soufflé-like dessert.

Rothenburg ob der Tauber, Germany:#

  • Treat your taste buds to “Bratwurst,” a grilled sausage served with sauerkraut and mashed potatoes.
  • Experience the flavors of “Nuremberg Gingerbread,” a traditional Christmas treat.
  • Enjoy a slice of “Lebkuchen,” a spiced honey cake, a favorite during the festive season.

Colmar, France:#

  • Delight in the richness of “Foie Gras,” a luxurious pâté made from duck or goose liver.
  • For a sweet treat, try “Bûche de Noël,” a chocolate log cake resembling a yule log.
  • Sip on “Vin Chaud,” a warm spiced wine similar to Glühwein.

Prague, Czech Republic:#

  • Sample “Trdelník,” a sweet pastry dough rolled around a stick and grilled.
  • Experience the taste of “Svíčková,” a tenderloin steak served in a creamy sauce with dumplings.
  • Enjoy a glass of “Svařák,” a traditional mulled wine served hot.

Brussels, Belgium:#

  • Savor the flavors of “Moules-frites,” steamed mussels served with French fries.
  • Indulge in the rich and creamy taste of “Carbonnades Flamandes,” a beef stew cooked in beer.
  • Treat yourself to a piece of “Speculoos,” a spiced shortbread cookie popular during the holiday season.

Key Takeaways:

  • Explore European destinations known for their unique local delicacies and seasonal menus.
  • Discover traditional Christmas dishes like Glühwein, Salzburger Nockerl, and Foie Gras.
  • Savory treats like Schnitzel, Bratwurst, and Moules-Frites are must-tries.
  • Experience festive desserts like Bûche de Noël, Lebkuchen, and Trdelník.
  • Indulge in warm mulled wine variations like Glühwein, Vin Chaud, and Svařák.

FAQ#

Q1: What makes Europe an ideal destination to travel in December?#

A1: Europe in December offers a magical atmosphere, festive holiday celebrations, charming Christmas markets, and enchanting winter landscapes, making it a captivating destination for travelers seeking unique and memorable experiences.

Q2: Which European cities are particularly enchanting during the Christmas season?#

A2: Some of the most enchanting European cities during the Christmas season include Prague with its festive markets, Vienna with its elegant decorations, and Rovaniemi in Finland, the hometown of Santa Claus.

Q3: Are there any unique cultural experiences or traditions associated with Christmas in Europe?#

A3: Yes, Europe is renowned for its diverse Christmas traditions, including traditional carols, delectable festive cuisine, vibrant parades, and cultural performances that vary from region to region.

Q4: How can I ensure a memorable and hassle-free trip to Europe in December?#

A4: Planning ahead is crucial. Research destinations, book accommodations and transportation early, pack appropriately for winter weather, and consider purchasing a European travel insurance policy for peace of mind.

Q5: What are some hidden gems or off-the-beaten-path destinations worth exploring in Europe during December?#

A5: Consider exploring picturesque villages like Hallstatt in Austria or Bled in Slovenia, known for their stunning natural beauty and festive ambiance. Alternatively, lesser-known Christmas markets in cities like Zagreb or Lviv offer unique cultural experiences.
