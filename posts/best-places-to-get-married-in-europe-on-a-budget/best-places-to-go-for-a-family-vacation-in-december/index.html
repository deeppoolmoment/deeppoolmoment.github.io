Best Places to Go for a Family Vacation in December#

Searching for the best places to go for a family vacation in December?

December is a magical time for family vacations, with twinkling lights, festive decorations, and the joy of the holiday season filling the air. From stunning winter wonderlands to tropical beach escapes, there are countless destinations that offer unforgettable experiences for families with children of all ages.

Gather your loved ones and embark on an adventure to one of these top destinations for a memorable December getaway:

1. Lapland, Finland: A Winter Wonderland#

  • Experience the magic of a snowy wonderland in Lapland, Finland, where the Northern Lights dance across the sky.
  • Take a thrilling reindeer sleigh ride, meet Santa Claus in his official hometown, and enjoy an array of festive activities, from ice skating to gingerbread decorating.

2. The Maldives: Tropical Paradise#

  • Escape to the tropical paradise of the Maldives, where pristine white-sand beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and luxurious resorts await.
  • Discover the underwater world by snorkeling or diving amidst colorful coral reefs teeming with marine life.
  • Relax and rejuvenate with family-friendly spa treatments and indulge in delectable cuisine.

3. Queenstown, New Zealand: Adventure Capital#

  • Embark on an adventure-filled vacation in Queenstown, New Zealand, renowned for its stunning scenery and thrilling activities.
  • Zip through canyons on a thrilling zip line, take a scenic gondola ride up Bob’s Peak, and embark on a family-friendly hike through breathtaking landscapes.

4. Vienna, Austria: Christmas Markets and Imperial Charm#

  • Immerse yourself in the grandeur of Vienna, Austria, during the holiday season, when the city transforms into a winter wonderland.
  • Explore the enchanting Christmas markets, sip hot chocolate while admiring the stunning architecture, and enjoy classical music concerts that fill the air with festive cheer.

5. Orlando, Florida: Theme Park Magic#

  • Experience the magic of Orlando, Florida, home to world-renowned theme parks like Walt Disney World, Universal Orlando Resort, and SeaWorld.
  • Meet your favorite characters, ride exhilarating rides, and create unforgettable memories that will last a lifetime.

6. Banff, Canada: Majestic Mountains#

  • Discover the stunning beauty of Banff National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its majestic mountains, turquoise lakes, and abundant wildlife.
  • Go skiing or snowboarding on world-class slopes, take a scenic gondola ride, and spot wildlife such as elk, deer, and bears.

7. Copenhagen, Denmark: Hygge and Fairy-tale Charm#

  • Experience the cozy charm of Copenhagen, Denmark, known for its “hygge” lifestyle and fairy-tale atmosphere.
  • Explore the colorful streets of Nyhavn, visit the iconic Tivoli Gardens amusement park, and indulge in delicious pastries and hot chocolate.

As you plan your best places to go for a family vacation in December, remember to consider factors such as the destination’s weather, family-friendly attractions, and accommodation options.

Prioritize safety and accessibility:#

Traveling with children often poses unique challenges. The last thing you want for your family trip is to face safety concerns and accessibility issues. If you’re a parent searching for hassle-free, memorable vacations in December, prioritize destinations that put safety and accessibility at the forefront.

Stay vigilant: Research destinations known for their low crime rates and robust tourism infrastructure. Read travel blogs and online reviews to gain insights into the local safety conditions.

Consider medical care: Check for the availability of pediatric healthcare facilities and English-speaking medical professionals. Travel with a basic first aid kit, essential medications, and copies of your children’s medical records.

Choose age-appropriate activities: Prioritize destinations with attractions and activities tailored to your family’s age groups. Ensure that amusement parks and hiking trails have options suitable for both younger and older children.

Plan accessible transportation: If you’re traveling with young children or individuals with disabilities, ensure the destination has accessible public transportation options or consider renting a wheelchair or stroller if needed.

Stay connected: Keep your mobile phones charged and have a local SIM card or international roaming plan. Download offline maps and emergency contact information before your trip. Check local laws and regulations regarding internet access and mobile phone usage.

Follow local customs and laws: Familiarize yourself with the local customs, laws, and dress codes. Respect cultural norms and be mindful of local sensitivities. Remember, simple gestures like learning basic phrases in the local language can go a long way in fostering positive interactions.

Key Takeaways:

  • Prioritize destinations with low crime rates and robust tourism infrastructure.
  • Research the availability of pediatric healthcare facilities and English-speaking medical professionals.
  • Choose age-appropriate activities and attractions to cater to different age groups.
  • Ensure accessible transportation options are available for young children or individuals with disabilities.
  • Stay connected by keeping your mobile phones charged and having a local SIM card or international roaming plan.
  • Respect local customs, laws, and dress codes.
  • Learn basic phrases in the local language to foster positive interactions.

Choose accommodations that cater to families#

Hotels or resorts that provide connecting rooms, family suites, or apartments with multiple bedrooms and bathrooms are ideal for families with young children. Larger accommodations provide ample space for everyone to spread out and feel comfortable, and they often include amenities like kitchens or kitchenettes, which can be convenient for preparing meals and snacks.

Look for accommodations with amenities that cater to families. This could include:

  • Cribs, Pack n’ Plays, and rollaway beds. These can be essential for families with young children.
  • A swimming pool, a playground, and a game room. These amenities can help keep children entertained on rainy days or when you need a break from sightseeing.
  • A babysitting service. This can be a lifesaver for parents who need a night out or a few hours to themselves.
  • A restaurant or cafe on-site. This can be convenient for families who don’t want to venture out for meals.
  • Free parking or shuttle service. This can be a big plus for families who are traveling by car.

Additionally, consider the location of the accommodations.

  • Choose a hotel or resort that is close to family-friendly attractions, restaurants, and shops. This will make it easy for you to get around and enjoy your vacation without having to rent a car or spend a lot of time on the road.
  • If you are planning on doing a lot of sightseeing, choose a hotel or resort that is centrally located. This will make it easy to get to the attractions you want to see.

Key Takeaways:

  • Select accommodations that offer connecting rooms, family suites, or apartments with multiple bedrooms and bathrooms.
  • Look for accommodations with family-friendly amenities like cribs, pack n’ plays, rollaway beds, swimming pools, playgrounds, game rooms, babysitting services, on-site restaurants, and free parking or shuttle services.
  • Consider the location of the accommodations, opting for those close to family-friendly attractions, restaurants, and shops, or centrally located for easy sightseeing.
  • Conduct thorough research, read reviews, and compare prices to find the best accommodations that suit your family’s needs and budget.
  • Make reservations well in advance, especially during peak seasons, to secure your preferred accommodations and avoid last-minute disappointments.

Plan ahead and book early#

In the realm of family vacations, planning and early booking are not mere suggestions but essential strategies for an unforgettable holiday experience. Here’s why:

  • Avoid the holiday rush: December is a prime time for family vacations, which means popular destinations are likely to be packed. Booking early allows you to secure accommodations, flights, and activities before they’re snatched up.

  • Competitive pricing: Early booking often comes with discounted rates and special offers. You might land a great deal on airfare, lodging, or family-friendly packages.

  • Peace of mind: Knowing everything is in place well in advance can alleviate stress and uncertainty, allowing you to focus on creating cherished memories with your loved ones.

  • Flexibility: Life happens, and sometimes plans change. If you book early, you’ll have more flexibility to adjust your itinerary or cancel without incurring significant penalties.

Prepare a Detailed Itinerary:

  • Research: Dive into research to identify destinations that align with your family’s interests and preferences. Explore attractions, activities, and accommodations that cater to all ages.

  • Create a daily schedule: Outline a tentative itinerary with activities, meal times, and downtime. This helps you make the most of your time and avoid feeling overwhelmed.

  • Consider weather conditions: December weather can be unpredictable, especially if you’re traveling to a destination with varying climates. Pack accordingly and have a backup plan in case of inclement weather.

Book Accommodations Wisely:

  • Options: Choose from various accommodation options: hotels, resorts, vacation rentals, or cozy cabins. Consider factors like location, proximity to attractions, amenities, and family-friendly features.

  • Amenities: Ensure the accommodations have amenities that cater to your family’s needs, such as kid-friendly pools, playgrounds, or babysitting services.

  • Reviews: Read online reviews from other families to gain insights into their experiences and make informed decisions.

  • Book early: Don’t wait until the last minute to book your accommodations, especially if you’re traveling during peak season. Popular hotels and resorts can fill up quickly.

Plan Activities and Attractions:

  • Variety: Offer a mix of activities and attractions that cater to different interests and age groups. Include historical sites, theme parks, museums, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences.

  • Online research: Utilize online platforms, travel blogs, and guidebooks to discover hidden gems and off-the-beaten-path attractions.

  • Consider accessibility: If you have young children or family members with mobility challenges, ensure the activities and attractions are accessible and inclusive.

  • Book tickets: Secure tickets for popular attractions and activities in advance, especially during peak season, to avoid long lines and disappointment.

Pack Smartly:

  • Layers: Dress in layers to adapt to varying temperatures, especially if you’re traveling to a destination with unpredictable weather.

  • Essentials: Pack essentials like toiletries, medications, chargers, and important documents. Don’t forget to include snacks and entertainment for the journey.

  • Entertainment: Bring along books, games, and portable devices to keep the kids occupied during downtime.

Be Flexible and Patient:

  • Adapt to changes: Things don’t always go according to plan, especially when traveling with kids. Be flexible and patient when unexpected situations arise.

  • Enjoy the journey: Embrace the journey as part of the adventure. Enjoy the moments together and make memories that will last a lifetime.

Key Takeaways:

  • Plan ahead and book early to secure the best deals, avoid crowds, and have peace of mind.
  • Create a detailed itinerary that caters to your family’s interests and preferences.
  • Book accommodations wisely, considering factors like location, amenities, and family-friendly features.
  • Plan activities and attractions that offer variety, accessibility, and cater to different age groups.
  • Pack smartly, including layers of clothing, essentials, entertainment, and snacks for the journey.
  • Be flexible and patient, adapting to changes and enjoying the journey as part of the adventure.
