Best Non Tourist Places in Ireland#

Ireland’s allure goes beyond its iconic cities and popular attractions. Venture off the beaten path and discover the hidden gems that embody Ireland’s true spirit. From charming villages to rugged coastlines, these non-touristy places offer a unique and authentic Irish experience.

Aran Islands, County Galway:#

Escape to the Aran Islands, a group of three islands off the coast of Galway. Discover the ancient Dún Aonghasa, a spectacular prehistoric fort perched on dramatic cliffs. Explore the tranquil beaches, wander through traditional villages, and immerse yourself in the rich Gaelic culture of the islands.

Glendalough, County Wicklow:#

Nestled in the picturesque Wicklow Mountains, Glendalough is a mesmerizing valley teeming with natural beauty and historical treasures. Hike amidst stunning scenery, marvel at the well-preserved monastic ruins, and delve into the captivating stories of St. Kevin, the founder of the monastery.

Derrynane, County Kerry:#

Discover the enchanting village of Derrynane, nestled along the stunning Iveragh Peninsula. Stroll along the golden sands of Derrynane Beach, visit the ancestral home of Daniel O’Connell, a prominent Irish leader, and immerse yourself in the breathtaking beauty of the surrounding countryside.

The Burren, County Clare:#

Explore the awe-inspiring Burren, a unique karst landscape characterized by dramatic limestone formations, wildflowers, and archaeological treasures. Wander through the lunar-like terrain, visit ancient dolmens and forts, and be captivated by the beauty of this natural wonder.

Slieve League, County Donegal:#

Experience the exhilarating Slieve League, one of Europe’s highest sea cliffs. Embark on a challenging hike along the cliff edge, marvel at the breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean, and soak in the unspoiled beauty of this rugged coastal region.

Connemara, County Galway:#

Immerse yourself in the wild beauty of Connemara, a region renowned for its rugged mountains, pristine lakes, and charming villages. Explore the picturesque Kylemore Abbey, visit the traditional fishing village of Roundstone, and be captivated by the stunning scenery that has inspired artists and writers for centuries.
