Best Places in Europe for Snow in December#

When snowflakes dance in December’s chilly breeze, Europe transforms into a snowy wonderland. From the glistening Alps to the frozen fjords, a world of winter magic awaits. So pack your bags, don your winter gear, and let’s embark on a journey to discover the best places in Europe for snow in December.

1. The Alps: A Ski Enthusiast’s Paradise#

Nestled amidst towering peaks and pristine valleys, the Alps offer a skier’s paradise. From the legendary slopes of Chamonix in France to the charming villages of St. Anton in Austria, this winter wonderland beckons. Glide down powdery slopes, soak in panoramic vistas, and experience the thrill of skiing in some of Europe’s most iconic ski resorts.

2. The Dolomites: A UNESCO World Heritage Site#

With its dramatic peaks, sheer rock faces, and picturesque valleys, the Dolomites in Italy offer a breathtaking backdrop for your winter adventure. Ski through UNESCO-protected landscapes, marvel at the unique geological formations, and indulge in traditional Italian cuisine in charming mountain villages.

3. Lapland: A Magical Arctic Adventure#

Venture above the Arctic Circle to Lapland, a land of snow-covered forests, frozen lakes, and dancing Northern Lights. Experience the thrill of dog-sledding, snowmobiling, and reindeer sleigh rides. Immerse yourself in the Sami culture, visit a reindeer farm, and witness the ethereal beauty of the Aurora Borealis.

4. Scandinavian Fjords: A Winter Fairytale#

Discover the serene beauty of the Scandinavian fjords, where snow-capped mountains meet icy waters. Explore the picturesque towns of Norway, Sweden, and Denmark, and witness breathtaking fjords adorned with cascading waterfalls and snow-covered peaks. Embark on a fjord cruise, go cross-country skiing, or simply relax in a cozy cabin by the fireplace.

5. The Black Forest: A Winter Retreat#

Immerse yourself in the enchanting Black Forest of Germany, a region steeped in folklore and natural beauty. Discover charming towns like Freiburg and Triberg, adorned with Christmas markets and festive decorations. Explore snow-covered forests on snowshoes, visit fairytale castles, and savor the region’s delectable Black Forest cake.

6. Iceland: A Land of Ice and Fire#

Experience the stark beauty of Iceland in December, where ice and fire coexist in perfect harmony. Discover glaciers, geysers, and cascading waterfalls, all blanketed in a layer of pristine snow. Go snowmobiling on glaciers, relax in geothermal hot springs, and witness the captivating Northern Lights.

7. Transylvania: A Winter Mystery#

Unravel the mysteries of Transylvania, Romania, a region shrouded in folklore and legends. Explore historic castles like Bran Castle, associated with Vlad the Impaler, also known as Dracula. Discover charming villages nestled amidst snow-covered mountains, and indulge in traditional Romanian cuisine.

Whether you seek adrenaline-pumping adventures or serene winter escapes, Europe has something for every snow-loving traveler. Unveiling Europe’s Snowy Treasures:

  1. Switzerland: A Skiing Paradise:

  • Embark on an unforgettable skiing adventure in the Swiss Alps, renowned for their world-class resorts and breathtaking scenery.

  • Explore the legendary slopes of St. Moritz, Zermatt, and Davos, offering terrain suitable for skiers of all levels.

  • Venture beyond skiing and indulge in winter hiking, snowshoeing, and traditional Swiss cheese fondue.

  1. Austria: Christmas Magic at its Finest:

  • Discover the charm of Austrian villages adorned with twinkling lights and festive decorations.

  • Visit Salzburg, the birthplace of Mozart, and immerse yourself in its rich musical heritage.

  • Take a romantic horse-drawn sleigh ride through snowy landscapes and indulge in traditional Austrian delicacies.

  1. Finland: Experience the Arctic Circle:

  • Journey to Finnish Lapland, home to the magical Northern Lights, and experience the tranquility of Arctic wilderness.

  • Embark on a husky safari, marvel at the beauty of frozen waterfalls, and try your hand at ice fishing.

  • Stay in a cozy glass igloo, offering a mesmerizing view of the night sky.

  1. Norway: Fjords and Snowy Peaks:

  • Explore the stunning Norwegian fjords, fringed by snow-capped mountains and cascading waterfalls.

  • Ski or snowboard down the slopes of Trysil, Norway’s largest ski resort, surrounded by breathtaking scenery.

  • Discover the vibrant city of Bergen, known for its colorful wooden houses and lively atmosphere.

  1. Iceland: Land of Fire and Ice:

  • Witness the awe-inspiring beauty of Iceland, where glaciers, volcanoes, and geysers coexist in harmony.

  • Go on a snowmobile adventure across frozen landscapes, marvel at the cascading Gullfoss waterfall, and relax in geothermal hot springs.

  • Explore the unique ice caves and witness the natural phenomenon of the Northern Lights.

Additional Tips for a Memorable Winter Journey:

  • Embrace the festive spirit by visiting Christmas markets, indulging in local delicacies, and attending traditional events.

  • Research local customs and traditions to enhance your understanding of the region’s culture.

  • Consider hiring a local guide to learn about hidden gems and off-the-beaten-path experiences.

  • Be prepared for unpredictable weather conditions by packing layers of clothing and essential gear.

  • Respect the environment and follow local guidelines to ensure responsible travel.
