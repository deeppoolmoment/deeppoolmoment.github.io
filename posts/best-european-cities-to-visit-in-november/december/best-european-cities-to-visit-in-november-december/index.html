Best European Cities to Visit in November/December

Exploring Europe during the festive season in November and December is an enchanting experience. As the continent transforms into a winter wonderland, many cities come alive with twinkling lights, festive markets, and joyous celebrations. Here are some of the best European cities to visit in November/December:

Prague, Czech Republic

Known for its stunning medieval architecture and rich history, Prague is a magical destination during the holiday season. Stroll through the enchanting Old Town Square, adorned with a majestic Christmas tree and surrounded by charming stalls selling traditional Czech delicacies and souvenirs. Don’t miss a visit to the Prague Castle Complex to witness stunning views of the city and immerse yourself in its captivating history.

Vienna, Austria

Vienna’s imperial grandeur and musical heritage make it an ideal destination for a festive getaway. Admire the festive decorations adorning the streets and indulge in traditional Viennese treats like Sachertorte and warm gluhwein. Attend a concert at the Musikverein or the Wiener Staatsoper, or take a leisurely stroll through the city’s iconic Schönbrunn Palace gardens.

Budapest, Hungary

Budapest’s thermal baths and vibrant atmosphere provide a unique winter escape. Discover the city’s architectural marvels, from the iconic Hungarian Parliament Building to Buda Castle. Stroll along the banks of the Danube River and take in the stunning city views. Enjoy the festive markets, ice skating rinks, and special events that bring the city to life during the holiday season.

Copenhagen, Denmark

Copenhagen exudes a cozy charm during the festive season. Explore the Tivoli Gardens, a historic amusement park transformed into a winter wonderland with twinkling lights, Christmas decorations, and festive rides. Enjoy a stroll along the Nyhavn canal, lined with colorful buildings and charming cafés. Don’t miss a visit to the city’s iconic Little Mermaid statue.

Reykjavik, Iceland

Experience a truly unique festive season in Reykjavik, where the city is illuminated by the Northern Lights. Take a dip in the geothermal Blue Lagoon, surrounded by a snowy landscape. Embark on a whale watching tour or visit the Golden Circle, a scenic route featuring natural wonders like Thingvellir National Park and the Gullfoss waterfall.

No matter which city you choose, the best european cities to visit in november/december offer a blend of festive cheer, cultural richness, and stunning winter scenery. Embrace the magic of the season and create lasting memories in these captivating European destinations.

1. Prague, Czech Republic:

  • Pros: Stunning architecture, Vibrant nightlife, Rich history, Affordable prices.
  • Cons: Crowded during peak season, Cold weather.
  • Recommendations: Explore the Old Town Square, visit Prague Castle, and take a stroll across Charles Bridge.
  • Tips: Dress warmly, try Trdelník (a traditional pastry), and enjoy the Christmas markets.

2. Vienna, Austria:

  • Pros: Elegant architecture, World-class museums, Classical music scene, Christmas markets.
  • Cons: Can be expensive, Crowded during peak season.
  • Recommendations: Visit the Schönbrunn Palace, attend a concert at the Musikverein, and explore the Weihnachtsmarkt am Rathausplatz.
  • Tips: Book tickets for attractions in advance, try Wiener Schnitzel (a traditional dish), and take advantage of the public transportation system.

3. Budapest, Hungary:

  • Pros: Thermal baths, Beautiful architecture, Affordable prices, Lively nightlife.
  • Cons: Cold weather, Pickpocketing can be an issue.
  • Recommendations: Visit the Széchenyi Thermal Baths, explore the Buda Castle, and take a river cruise on the Danube.
  • Tips: Dress warmly, try Goulash (a traditional stew), and take advantage of the ruin bars.

4. Edinburgh, Scotland:

  • Pros: Historic castle, Vibrant culture, Christmas markets, Hogmanay celebrations.
  • Cons: Cold weather, Crowded during peak season.
  • Recommendations: Visit Edinburgh Castle, explore the Royal Mile, and enjoy the Christmas markets.
  • Tips: Dress warmly, try Haggis (a traditional dish), and experience the unique Hogmanay celebrations.

5. Amsterdam, Netherlands:

  • Pros: Canals, Museums, Red-light district, Christmas markets.
  • Cons: Crowded during peak season, Can be expensive.
  • Recommendations: Take a canal boat tour, visit the Rijksmuseum, and explore the Christmas markets.
  • Tips: Dress warmly, try Poffertjes (a traditional pancake), and enjoy the lively atmosphere.

November and December bring a magical charm to Europe, with festive markets, twinkling lights, and an atmosphere that’s simply enchanting. If you’re planning a winter getaway, here are some of the best European cities to visit during this special time of year:

Prague, Czech Republic#

Prague is a city that oozes charm and romance, and it’s even more magical during the festive season. The Old Town Square is transformed into a winter wonderland, with a huge Christmas tree, a traditional market, and plenty of festive decorations. Be sure to try some of the local mulled wine and indulge in some delicious Czech pastries.

Budapest, Hungary#

Budapest is another city that comes alive during the festive season. The streets are lined with twinkling lights, and the Christmas markets are a great place to pick up unique gifts and souvenirs. Don’t miss a visit to the famous Szechenyi Thermal Baths, where you can relax and soak away your troubles.

Copenhagen, Denmark#

Copenhagen is a city that knows how to celebrate Christmas. The city is decorated with festive lights and decorations, and there are plenty of Christmas markets to explore. Be sure to visit Tivoli Gardens, one of the oldest and most famous amusement parks in the world, which is transformed into a winter wonderland during the festive season.

Vienna, Austria#

Vienna is a city of music and culture, and it’s the perfect place to spend the festive season. The city is home to some of the world’s best Christmas markets, and there are plenty of concerts and performances to enjoy. Be sure to visit the Schönbrunn Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which is beautifully decorated for the festive season.

Berlin, Germany#

Berlin is a city that knows how to party, and the festive season is no exception. The city is home to some of the best Christmas markets in Europe, and there are plenty of bars and clubs to keep you entertained. Be sure to visit the Brandenburg Gate, one of the city’s most iconic landmarks, which is illuminated with festive lights during the festive season.

Key Takeaways:

  • Prague, Budapest, Copenhagen, Vienna, and Berlin are some of the best European cities to visit in November and December.
  • These cities offer a variety of festive attractions, including Christmas markets, twinkling lights, and special events.
  • Be sure to try some of the local mulled wine and pastries while you’re there.
  • You can find great deals on flights and accommodations if you book in advance.
