Best Family Holiday Resorts UK: A Guide to Unforgettable Experiences#

Hello there, fellow travel enthusiasts! As an experienced travel writer with a knack for family-friendly destinations, I’m delighted to share my insights on the best family holiday resorts in the UK. With two little ones of my own, I know firsthand what makes a vacation truly memorable for families. So, pack your bags, grab your sunscreen, and let’s embark on a journey to discover the UK’s hidden gems.

Devon Cliffs Holiday Park: Nestled along the stunning South Devon coast, Devon Cliffs is a haven for families seeking adventure. With its direct beach access, indoor and outdoor pools, and an array of kids’ clubs, boredom is never an option. Let the little ones make a splash at the Splash Zone, while you soak up the sun on the golden sands.

Bluestone National Park Resort: Immerse yourself in the beauty of Pembrokeshire at Bluestone. Set amidst lush woodland, this resort offers a magical escape for families. Think cozy lodges, a waterpark, an adventure playground, and a dedicated Treehouse Club. Embark on enchanting woodland walks, or let the kids run free in the great outdoors.

Haven Lakeland Village: Picture a holiday surrounded by the tranquil waters of Lake Windermere. Haven Lakeland Village is your idyllic base for exploring the Lake District. Its heated indoor and outdoor pools, exhilarating zip line, and range of family activities promise endless entertainment. Create cherished memories as you take a family cruise on Lake Windermere or embark on a scenic hike.

Center Parcs Longleat Forest: Prepare for a wild adventure at Center Parcs Longleat Forest in Wiltshire. This resort offers a unique blend of indoor and outdoor experiences. Stay in a woodland lodge, cycle through the forest, and meet adorable animals at the Longleat Safari Park. The Subtropical Swimming Paradise is a tropical oasis with water slides, wave pools, and plenty of splashy fun.

Port Lympne Reserve: Discover the wonders of the animal kingdom at Port Lympne Reserve in Kent. This wildlife park and hotel offer a unique opportunity to stay among exotic animals. Let the kids embark on a safari adventure, encountering giraffes, rhinos, and zebras. Explore the Dinosaur Forest, where life-sized dinosaurs roam, or take a thrilling boat trip on the African Experience.

Planning Tips:

  • Choose the Right Resort: Consider your family’s interests and needs. Whether you prefer a beachside escape, a countryside retreat, or an action-packed adventure, there’s a resort that ticks all the boxes.
  • Pack Wisely: Pack light but don’t forget the essentials. Swimsuits, sunscreen, and comfortable clothing are a must. Consider weather conditions and pack accordingly.
  • Make the Most of the Experience: Embrace the opportunity to bond as a family. Plan activities that everyone will enjoy, from sandcastle building to exploring local attractions. Create memories that will last a lifetime.

Unique Attractions and Features of Each Resort#

When choosing a family holiday resort in the UK, it’s the unique attractions and features that make all the difference. From thrilling theme parks to enchanting castles and captivating wildlife experiences, the UK has it all. Here are some of the most outstanding attractions and features that set each resort apart:

Adventure Parks:#

  • Alton Towers: Unleash your inner thrill-seeker at Alton Towers, home to some of the UK’s most exhilarating roller coasters and rides.
  • Oakwood Theme Park: Experience a blend of classic and modern attractions, including water rides, roller coasters, and live shows.
  • Thorpe Park: Dive into a world of high-octane rides, including the iconic Stealth and Colossus, set against a backdrop of stunning scenery.

Animal Encounters:#

  • Chester Zoo: Embark on a global wildlife adventure at Chester Zoo, home to over 20,000 animals from around the world.
  • Zoological Society of London (ZSL) London Zoo: Immerse yourself in the fascinating world of wildlife at ZSL London Zoo, the world’s oldest scientific zoo.
  • Paignton Zoo: Explore the diverse animal kingdom at Paignton Zoo, with a focus on conservation and endangered species.

Heritage and Culture:#

  • York: Journey through time in the medieval city of York, with its awe-inspiring York Minster, fascinating museums, and captivating historical attractions.
  • Edinburgh: Discover the rich history and culture of Edinburgh, with its iconic castle, cobblestone streets, and world-class art festivals.
  • Oxford: Delve into the academic and historical heart of Oxford, with its prestigious university, stunning architecture, and beautiful parks.

Coastal Delights:#

  • Cornwall: Bask in the sun-kissed beaches and rugged coastline of Cornwall, known for its surfing, scenic walks, and traditional Cornish pasties.
  • Devon: Explore the picturesque coastline of Devon, with its charming seaside towns, dramatic cliffs, and delightful cream teas.
  • Isle of Wight: Embrace the tranquil beauty of the Isle of Wight, offering sandy beaches, picturesque villages, and scenic walks.

Natural Wonders:#

  • Lake District: Escape to the breathtaking landscapes of the Lake District, with its tranquil lakes, towering mountains, and charming villages.
  • Peak District: Discover the natural beauty of the Peak District, renowned for its rolling hills, caves, and picturesque villages.
  • New Forest: Embark on a woodland adventure in the New Forest, with its ancient trees, roaming ponies, and charming villages.

Key Takeaways:

  • The UK offers a diverse range of family holiday resorts, each with unique attractions and features.
  • From adventure parks and animal encounters to heritage sites and coastal delights, there’s something for everyone.
  • Whether you seek thrilling rides, fascinating wildlife, historical exploration, or coastal adventures, the UK has it all.
  • Consider your family’s interests and preferences when choosing a resort to ensure a memorable and enjoyable holiday experience.

Practical Tips and Advice for Planning a Memorable Trip#

As an experienced travel writer, I’ve reveled in the joy of family travel, exploring the best family holiday resorts in the UK. With dozens of resorts reviewed, I’ve honed my eye for details that make a holiday truly memorable. As a parent of two young kids, I understand firsthand what families seek in a resort. Allow me to guide you through a journey of unforgettable family vacations.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Proper Planning: Lay the groundwork for a seamless trip by meticulously planning your itinerary.
  • Early Booking: Seize early booking discounts to bag the best deals on accommodations and activities.
  • Pack Smart: Prioritize essentials and pack light to avoid unnecessary luggage hassle.
  • Prepare Kids: Engage children in trip planning to stir anticipation and make them invested in the journey.
  • Flexibility: Embrace flexibility to accommodate unexpected changes and make room for spontaneous discoveries.

In-depth Planning:#

  • Research, Research, Research: Dive deep into online reviews and recommendations, seeking insights from seasoned travelers who have experienced the resorts firsthand.
  • Consult the Experts: Don’t shy away from seeking advice from travel agents specializing in family vacations. Their expertise can unveil hidden gems and exclusive deals.
  • Tailor Your Itinerary: Design an itinerary that caters to the interests of every family member, striking a balance between adult relaxation and kid-friendly fun.
  • Prioritize Safety: Ensure the resort has impeccable safety standards, particularly if you have young children. Look for amenities like supervised kids’ clubs and lifeguards.

Early Booking:#

  • Unlock Savings: Bag significant savings by booking your trip well in advance. Resorts often offer early bird discounts to entice bookings.
  • Secure Accommodation: Popular family resorts tend to fill up quickly, so early booking ensures you secure your preferred accommodation type.
  • Choice Activities: Booking early also gives you the first pick of activities and excursions, allowing you to avoid disappointment.

Smart Packing:#

  • Essentials First: Prioritize packing necessities like toiletries, medications, and essential clothing. Leave behind non-essentials to minimize luggage weight.
  • Layering is Key: Pack clothing layers to adapt to changing weather conditions, ensuring comfort and versatility.
  • Entertainment Essentials: Keep kids occupied during travel with portable entertainment options like books, games, and tablets.
  • Travel-sized Toiletries: Save space and comply with carry-on restrictions by opting for travel-sized toiletries.

Engaging Kids:#

  • Involve Them: Engage children in the trip planning process. Let them contribute ideas for activities and destinations, fostering a sense of ownership and excitement.
  • Build Anticipation: Share travel brochures and online videos about the destination to build anticipation and excitement among the kids.
  • Interactive Games: Turn the journey into an interactive game by playing car games or scavenger hunts to keep kids entertained during travel.

Flexibility:#

  • Embrace the Unexpected: Be open to unexpected changes in plans due to weather or unforeseen circumstances. Embrace these moments as opportunities for spontaneous discoveries.
  • Plan Leisure Time: Allow ample time for relaxation and unplanned activities. Sometimes, the most memorable moments arise from unplanned adventures.
  • Don’t Overpack the Schedule: Avoid cramming your itinerary with too many activities. Leave room for downtime to recharge and savor the moments.

Recommendations for Choosing the Right Resort and Packing Essentials#

Hello there, my fellow travel enthusiasts! As a seasoned travel writer who specializes in family vacations, I’m here to guide you through the exciting world of family resorts in the UK. Whether you seek seaside escapes or countryside retreats, I’ve got you covered.

When it comes to selecting the perfect family resort, it’s all about matching your needs and preferences with the resort’s offerings. Here are some key considerations:

  • Resort Location: Think about the destination you want to visit and choose a resort that’s conveniently located near attractions and activities that interest your family. If you’re a beach lover, opt for a seaside resort. If you prefer countryside adventures, go for a resort nestled amid rolling hills or national parks.

  • Resort Amenities: Check what facilities and services the resort offers. Are there pools, kids’ clubs, playgrounds, and other amenities catering to families? Does it have restaurants, spas, and fitness centers for adult relaxation? Ensure the resort has everything you need for a comfortable and enjoyable stay.

  • Accommodation Options: Consider the types of accommodation available at the resort. Are there family-friendly rooms or suites with enough space for everyone? Do they provide cribs, high chairs, and other essentials for little ones? Choose a resort that offers accommodations tailored to families’ needs.

  • Activities and Entertainment: Look into the activities and entertainment options available at the resort. Are there organized activities like kids’ clubs, sports tournaments, or themed nights? Does the resort have facilities for activities like swimming, biking, hiking, or gaming? Choose a resort that offers activities that appeal to all family members.

  • Read Reviews: Before making a decision, take the time to read reviews from other families who have stayed at the resort. This can give you valuable insights into the resort’s strengths and weaknesses, helping you make an informed choice.

Now, let’s talk about the packing essentials for your family holiday:

  • Clothing: Pack clothes suitable for the resort’s location and activities. Remember to include swimwear, sunscreens, hats, and sunglasses if you’re heading to a beach resort. If you’re planning outdoor adventures, pack hiking shoes, raincoats, and layers for changing weather conditions.

  • Toiletries: Don’t forget to pack toiletries for everyone in the family, including shampoo, conditioner, soap, toothpaste, and other essentials. Consider packing travel-sized toiletries to save space in your luggage.

  • Medications: If anyone in the family takes medications, ensure you pack an adequate supply for the duration of your trip, including any prescription drugs and over-the-counter medicines.

  • Entertainment: Pack books, games, toys, and other entertainment options to keep the kids occupied during downtime at the resort. You might also want to consider downloading movies or TV shows to your devices for long journeys.

  • Important Documents: Don’t forget to pack important documents like passports, visas, travel insurance, and hotel reservations. Keep these documents organized and easily accessible throughout your trip.

By following these recommendations and packing essentials, you can ensure that your family holiday to a UK resort is a memorable and stress-free experience. Happy travels!

FAQ#

Q1: What are the best family holiday resorts in the UK?#

A1: The best family holiday resorts in the UK offer a wide range of activities and amenities for families of all ages. Some of the most popular resorts include Centre Parcs, Butlin’s, Haven Holidays, and Pontins. These resorts typically have swimming pools, playgrounds, kids’ clubs, and restaurants. Many also offer activities such as bowling, mini-golf, and go-karting.

Q2: What are some of the best family holiday destinations in the UK?#

A2: Some of the best family holiday destinations in the UK include seaside resorts such as Blackpool, Brighton, and Bournemouth. These resorts offer a variety of attractions for families, such as beaches, amusement parks, and museums. Other popular family holiday destinations include the Lake District, the Cotswolds, and the Yorkshire Dales. These areas offer stunning scenery, walking trails, and family-friendly attractions.

Q3: What are some of the best things to do with kids in the UK?#

A3: There are many fun and exciting things to do with kids in the UK. Some of the most popular activities include visiting theme parks, zoos, and museums. There are also many beautiful parks and gardens that are perfect for a family picnic or a leisurely stroll. If you’re looking for something more adventurous, you can go hiking, biking, or camping.

Q4: What are some tips for planning a family holiday in the UK?#

A4: When planning a family holiday in the UK, it’s important to consider the ages of your children and their interests. You should also think about the time of year you’re traveling and the budget you have. Once you’ve considered these factors, you can start narrowing down your options. It’s also a good idea to book your accommodation and activities in advance, especially if you’re traveling during peak season.

Q5: What are some of the best family-friendly restaurants in the UK?#

A5: There are many family-friendly restaurants in the UK that offer a variety of cuisines and atmospheres. Some of the most popular options include chain restaurants such as Pizza Express and Nando’s. There are also many independent restaurants that cater to families, such as The Real Greek and Wahaca. When choosing a restaurant, it’s important to consider the ages of your children and their dietary needs.
