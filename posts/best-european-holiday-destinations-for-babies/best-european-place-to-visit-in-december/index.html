Best European Places to Visit in December#

Europe is a continent that has it all – fascinating history, breathtaking landscapes, vibrant culture, and oh, the delectable food! In December, Europe is a true winter wonderland, adorned with twinkling lights, festive markets, and an electrifying atmosphere. From snow-capped mountains to charming medieval towns, let’s embark on a journey to discover the magical destinations that await you in Europe this December.

Prague, Czech Republic: A Fairytale Come to Life#

Imagine a city where Gothic spires pierce the sky, cobblestone streets invite you to stroll, and the aroma of roasted chestnuts fills the air. That’s Prague, a city that’s straight out of a fairytale. At the heart of it all is the Old Town Square, where you can witness the magical spectacle of the Christmas market. Its wooden stalls overflow with hand-crafted ornaments, mulled wine warms your hands, and the scent of gingerbread wafts through the air. Prague in December is like a scene from a timeless storybook.

Zagreb, Croatia: A City of Lights#

Zagreb, Croatia, is famous for having the best Christmas lights in Europe. The city puts up such a dazzling display of lights that they can be seen from miles away. The best way to see them is to take a walk through the city center, where you can admire the sparkling decorations and enjoy the festive atmosphere.

Finland: The Land of Santa Claus#

Finland is known by many as the official home of Santa Claus, and what better time to visit the land of Santa than in December? From the snow-laden forests of Lapland to the enchanting village of Santa Claus, Finland embraces the magic of Christmas like no other place on Earth. Glide through the snowy wilderness on a reindeer-pulled sleigh, meet the jolly old Saint Nick himself, and soak in the beauty of the Northern Lights.

Vienna, Austria: The Sound of the Season#

Vienna is renowned for its classical music, and during the holiday season, the city is alive with the melodies of Christmas concerts. Attend a performance at the Musikverein or the State Opera House and let the music elevate your spirits. Explore the grand palaces and museums by day, and in the evening, revel in the merriment of the Christmas markets, indulging in sweet treats and exchanging gifts with loved ones.

Budapest, Hungary: Thermal Baths and Festive Delights#

Budapest is a city that blends history, culture, and natural beauty in perfect harmony. In December, the streets twinkle with festive lights, and the air is filled with the scent of roasted chestnuts. Stroll along the banks of the Danube River, admiring the illuminated bridges and buildings. Soak in the warmth of the thermal baths and enjoy the sheer bliss of relaxation. Budapest is a city that knows how to celebrate the season!

Copenhagen, Denmark: A Winter Wonderland#

Copenhagen is a city that exudes a cozy charm during the winter months. The charming streets are lined with festive decorations, and the Christmas markets are brimming with local treasures. Visit Tivoli Gardens, one of the world’s oldest amusement parks, transformed into a winter wonderland with twinkling lights, ice-skating rinks, and enchanting rides. Wrap up warm and take a stroll along the picturesque canals, relishing the beauty of the city’s iconic buildings.

Embrace the magic of Europe in December. From the snowy slopes of the Alps to the quaint cobblestone streets of medieval towns, this continent is a treasure trove of festive experiences. Let Europe be your winter wonderland, and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Cultural Celebrations: Immerse yourself in cultural celebrations like the Finnish Christmas sauna tradition, the Spanish Three Kings Parade, or the German Christkindlesmarkt.#

From the grand Christmas markets of Germany to the cozy sauna traditions of Finland, Europe offers a rich tapestry of cultural experiences during the festive month of December.

Step into a winter wonderland transformed by twinkling lights, festive decorations, and the sweet scent of mulled wine.

From historic cities adorned with holiday cheer to charming villages blanketed in snow, each destination unfolds a unique story of joy, tradition, and celebration.

Discover the magic of Europe this December and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Germany: Christkindlesmarkt (Christmas Market)#

Immerse yourself in the enchanting atmosphere of Germany’s Christmas markets.

From the world-famous Nuremberg Christkindlesmarkt to the picturesque market in Rothenburg ob der Tauber, these markets are a feast for the senses.

Find unique handcrafted gifts, sip on warm Glühwein, and savor delicious treats like Lebkuchen and Stollen. Don’t miss the chance to take a nostalgic ride on a horse-drawn carriage or listen to traditional Christmas carols sung by local choirs.

Finland: Christmas Sauna Tradition#

Experience the unique Finnish tradition of Christmas sauna.

Indulge in the invigorating heat of a traditional wooden sauna, followed by a refreshing dip in icy waters.

This centuries-old ritual is said to cleanse both body and soul, leaving you feeling refreshed and rejuvenated for the festive season ahead.

As you relax in the sauna, take a moment to appreciate the beauty of the Finnish winter landscape and the tranquility of the surrounding forests.

Spain: Three Kings Parade (Cabalgata de los Reyes Magos)#

Witness the spectacle of the Three Kings Parade in Spain, a grand procession that takes place on January 5th, the eve of Epiphany.

Elaborate floats carrying the Three Wise Men make their way through the streets, accompanied by marching bands, dancing troupes, and colorful costumes.

Children eagerly await the arrival of the Three Kings, who shower candy and gifts upon the cheering crowds.

The parade is a vibrant celebration of tradition, faith, and the joy of gift-giving.

Historical Sites: Visit iconic landmarks like the Eiffel Tower in Paris illuminated with festive lights, or explore the historic castles and palaces of Central Europe adorned with holiday decorations.#

Have you imagined strolling through the snow-covered streets of Europe, admiring iconic landmarks adorned with festive lights? December is a magical time to visit Europe, as the holiday spirit fills the air, and historical sites come alive with enchanting decorations.

Stroll Down the Champs-Élysées in Paris:

  • Picture yourself walking beneath the twinkling lights of the Champs-Élysées, surrounded by festive decorations and the buzz of holiday shoppers.
  • Don’t miss the awe-inspiring sight of the Eiffel Tower, illuminated in its holiday splendor, as you sip on a warm cup of vin chaud.

Explore the Historic Castles of Bavaria:

  • Travel to Bavaria, Germany, and immerse yourself in the fairy-tale charm of its historic castles.
  • Neuschwanstein Castle: Admire the grandeur of Neuschwanstein Castle, perched atop a hill overlooking stunning Alpine scenery.
  • Hohenschwangau Castle: Discover the rich history of Hohenschwangau Castle, the childhood home of King Ludwig II of Bavaria.

Uncover the Architectural Treasures of Prague:

  • Step into the heart of Prague, Czech Republic, and marvel at the architectural wonders of Old Town Square.
  • Prague Castle: Explore the majestic Prague Castle, a symbol of Bohemian history, and witness the changing of the guard ceremony.
  • Charles Bridge: Stroll across the Charles Bridge, adorned with statues of saints, and take in the panoramic views of the city.

Immerse Yourself in the History of Vienna:

  • Explore Vienna, Austria, and delve into its imperial past at the Schönbrunn Palace.
  • Schönbrunn Palace: Wander through the opulent halls of Schönbrunn Palace, once home to the Habsburg dynasty, and admire its stunning gardens.
  • Hofburg Palace: Visit the grand Hofburg Palace, the former residence of the Austrian emperors, and learn about the rich history of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.

Key Takeaways:

  • Paris: Stroll down the Champs-Élysées, admire the Eiffel Tower, and sip on vin chaud.
  • Bavaria: Explore the fairy-tale castles of Neuschwanstein and Hohenschwangau, and immerse yourself in the rich history of the region.
  • Prague: Discover the architectural treasures of Old Town Square, visit Prague Castle, and stroll across the Charles Bridge.
  • Vienna: Explore the Schönbrunn Palace, once home to the Habsburg dynasty, and delve into the imperial history of the city at the Hofburg Palace.

1. Austrian Alps: A Winter Wonderland#

Picture yourself nestled in a charming chalet, surrounded by snow-capped peaks and the crisp Alpine air. The Austrian Alps transform into a winter wonderland, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and festive charm.

  • Must-See Attractions: Embark on a sleigh ride through picturesque villages, visit traditional Christmas markets, and indulge in local delicacies like mulled wine and gingerbread. Don’t miss the awe-inspiring ice caves and the chance to witness the stunning aurora borealis.
  • Festive Events: Join the locals in celebrating unique customs like Krampuslauf, where masked figures parade through the streets, or attend festive concerts and events in historic towns like Salzburg and Innsbruck.
  • Cozy Accommodation: Choose from cozy chalets with fireplaces, panoramic views, and private saunas to luxurious resorts offering world-class amenities and festive packages.

2. Scottish Highlands: A Cozy Retreat#

Escape to the tranquil Scottish Highlands, where cozy guesthouses and inns await you amidst breathtaking landscapes. Immerse yourself in the region’s rich history, culture, and natural beauty.

  • Must-See Attractions: Visit ancient castles, explore the rugged coastline, and embark on scenic hikes through the glens. Don’t miss the chance to spot majestic wildlife like red deer, golden eagles, and seals.
  • Festive Events: Experience traditional Scottish Christmas celebrations, including ceilidhs (traditional Scottish dances), festive markets, and Highland Games. Indulge in local delicacies like haggis, neeps, and tatties, and raise a glass of Scotch whisky to toast the season.
  • Cozy Accommodation: Find refuge in charming guesthouses with roaring fireplaces, tartan decor, and hearty Scottish breakfasts. Secluded lodges with hot tubs and stunning views of the Highlands are also available for a truly unforgettable stay.

Experience the magic of Christmas in Europe’s iconic cities, where grand boulevards are illuminated with dazzling lights, and festive markets fill the air with the aroma of roasted chestnuts and mulled wine.

  • Must-See Attractions: Visit world-renowned landmarks, explore historic districts, and indulge in shopping for unique gifts and souvenirs. Don’t miss the spectacular Christmas light displays, ice-skating rinks, and festive concerts that bring these cities to life.
  • Festive Events: Immerse yourself in local traditions and celebrations, such as attending Christmas mass in grand cathedrals, participating in festive parades, or witnessing the lighting of the city’s Christmas tree.
  • Luxurious Accommodation: Stay in opulent hotels adorned with festive decorations, offering exceptional amenities, gourmet dining, and breathtaking views of the city’s skyline. Embrace the festive spirit with special holiday packages and festive room service menus.

FAQ#

Q1: What are the most popular European destinations for a December holiday?#

A1: Among the top European destinations for a December holiday are Prague, Vienna, Budapest, Bruges, and Edinburgh. These cities offer a captivating blend of festive markets, enchanting decorations, and cultural experiences that create a magical holiday atmosphere.

Q2: What are some of the must-see attractions in these destinations during December?#

A2: In Prague, don’t miss the Old Town Square Christmas Market, the Astronomical Clock, and the Charles Bridge. Vienna offers the Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz, Schönbrunn Palace, and the Belvedere Palace. Budapest features the Christmas Market at Vörösmarty Square, the Hungarian Parliament Building, and the Buda Castle. Bruges boasts the Winter Glow Festival, the Markt, and the Groeningemuseum. Edinburgh showcases the Christmas Market on Princes Street, Edinburgh Castle, and the Royal Mile.

Q3: What are some unique holiday traditions and events to experience in these destinations?#

A3: In Prague, witness the lighting of the Christmas tree in Old Town Square and enjoy traditional Czech Christmas carols. Vienna hosts the New Year’s Concert at the Musikverein and offers ice skating at the Rathausplatz. Budapest presents the Advent Singing Festival at St. Stephen’s Basilica and the Christmas Eve fireworks over the Danube River. Bruges captivates with its ice-skating rink on the Markt and the annual Christmas procession. Edinburgh celebrates Hogmanay, a Scottish New Year’s Eve festival, with torchlight processions, ceilidhs, and fireworks.

Q4: Where can I find the best Christmas markets in these destinations?#

A4: Prague’s Old Town Square Christmas Market is renowned for its traditional crafts, mulled wine, and festive atmosphere. Vienna’s Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz offers a wide variety of gifts, decorations, and culinary delights. Budapest’s Christmas Market at Vörösmarty Square showcases Hungarian handicrafts, traditional foods, and live music. Bruges’ Winter Glow Festival features a charming market with handmade gifts, local produce, and delicious treats. Edinburgh’s Christmas Market on Princes Street presents a festive atmosphere with wooden chalets selling unique gifts and treats.

Q5: How can I make the most of my December holiday in these European destinations?#

A5: To make the most of your December holiday, plan your itinerary carefully to include visits to the must-see attractions and experience unique holiday traditions. Immerse yourself in the festive atmosphere by attending Christmas markets, ice-skating rinks, and special events. Don’t forget to try local delicacies and warm up with a mug of mulled wine or hot chocolate. Remember to dress warmly and bring comfortable shoes for exploring the cities’ charming streets and squares.
