Best Place to Travel in Europe in December#

Winter wonderlands, charming Christmas markets, and festive celebrations await you in Europe in December. Whether you seek snow-capped mountains for skiing and snowboarding, enchanting cities adorned with twinkling lights, or cozy villages where traditions come alive, the continent offers a wealth of experiences.

Lapland, Finland#

For an Arctic adventure, head to Lapland, Finland, the home of Santa Claus. Explore the snow-covered forests on a husky safari, go ice fishing, and witness the awe-inspiring Northern Lights dancing across the sky.

Reykjavik, Iceland#

Iceland is a winter paradise with spectacular landscapes and unique experiences. Discover the geothermal wonders of the Blue Lagoon, go whale watching, and soak in the warm waters of natural hot springs.

Prague, Czech Republic#

Prague transforms into a magical winter wonderland during December. Stroll through the Old Town Square, adorned with Christmas decorations and a giant tree, and indulge in traditional Czech delicacies like trdelník and mulled wine.

Copenhagen, Denmark#

Copenhagen is known for its cozy atmosphere and hygge lifestyle. Visit the Tivoli Gardens, one of the oldest amusement parks in the world, which transforms into a winter wonderland with twinkling lights and festive decorations.

Vienna, Austria#

Vienna is renowned for its imperial grandeur and rich musical heritage. Attend a classical concert at the Musikverein, visit the Schönbrunn Palace, and explore the festive markets selling handmade gifts and delicious treats.

Zell am See, Austria#

Nestled in the heart of the Austrian Alps, Zell am See is a skier’s paradise. Enjoy world-class skiing and snowboarding on perfectly groomed slopes, and take in the breathtaking views of the snow-capped mountains.

Budapest, Hungary#

Budapest is a vibrant city with a rich history and thermal baths. Visit the Széchenyi Thermal Baths, one of the largest thermal baths in Europe, and explore the Buda Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Bruges, Belgium#

Bruges is a medieval city with a charming atmosphere. Wander through the cobbled streets, admire the picturesque canals, and indulge in Belgian chocolates and waffles.

Rovaniemi, Finland#

Rovaniemi is the official hometown of Santa Claus. Visit the Santa Claus Village, send a letter from the Santa Claus Post Office, and embark on a reindeer or husky safari through the snowy forests.

Innsbruck, Austria#

Innsbruck is a beautiful city surrounded by the stunning Austrian Alps. Explore the historic Old Town, visit the Hofburg Imperial Palace, and take a cable car up to the Nordkette mountains for panoramic views.

Snowy Adventures in the Alps#

Hello, travel enthusiasts! Ready to explore the breathtaking Alps for a snowy escapade? Picture yourself gliding down pristine slopes, surrounded by snow-capped peaks and picturesque villages. Let’s embark on a journey through the best winter destinations in the Alps, creating unforgettable memories.

Prestigious St. Anton#

St. Anton am Arlberg, Austria, is a legendary ski resort with over 300 kilometers of slopes. Take on exhilarating runs, from gentle beginner trails to challenging off-piste adventures. Afterward, unwind in charming Tyrolean chalets, indulging in delectable local cuisine.

Scenic Courchevel#

In the heart of the French Alps, Courchevel boasts world-class skiing terrain, catering to all skill levels. Experience the thrill of skiing amidst stunning scenery, including the legendary La Saulire, offering breathtaking panoramic views. Don’t miss the opportunity to soak in the region’s vibrant nightlife and indulge in gourmet dining at Michelin-starred restaurants.

Unforgettable Gstaad#

Nestled in the Bernese Oberland region of Switzerland, Gstaad exudes elegance and charm. Explore its pristine slopes, offering both challenging and leisurely trails. Embrace the après-ski culture, indulging in unforgettable culinary experiences and lively entertainment options. Whether you’re a seasoned skier or simply seeking a serene winter getaway, Gstaad has something for everyone.

Serene Zermatt#

Zermatt, Switzerland, offers an awe-inspiring backdrop of the Matterhorn. Immerse yourself in skiing amidst snowy landscapes, enjoying varied terrain suitable for skiers of all levels. Take a break from skiing and hop on the scenic Gornergrat Railway, leading to the summit of Gornergrat, where you can witness the majestic Matterhorn up close.

Alluring Val d’Isère#

Nestled in the Tarentaise Valley, France, Val d’Isère is a winter sports haven. Embark on exhilarating runs on its extensive slopes, catering to all levels of expertise. Discover hidden powder stashes for an unforgettable off-piste experience. Indulge in traditional Savoyard cuisine and immerse yourself in the lively après-ski scene, making Val d’Isère a vibrant destination for winter enthusiasts.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Discover the stunning landscapes and pristine slopes of St. Anton am Arlberg, Austria.
  • Experience the grandeur of Courchevel, France, with its world-class skiing terrain and vibrant nightlife.
  • Embrace the elegance and charm of Gstaad, Switzerland, offering serene slopes and unforgettable après-ski experiences.
  • Immerse yourself in the breathtaking scenery of Zermatt, Switzerland, and ski amidst the iconic Matterhorn.
  • Explore the extensive slopes and off-piste adventures in Val d’Isère, France, while enjoying its vibrant après-ski scene.

Embark on a snowy adventure in the magnificent Alps this winter. Whether you seek thrilling ski runs, picturesque villages, or unforgettable après-ski experiences, these destinations offer something for every traveler. Embrace the beauty and magic of the Alps, creating lasting memories that will stay with you long after your journey ends.

Cultural Celebrations and Traditions#

The Christmas markets that pop up all over Europe during December emanate a magical ambiance with their twinkling lights, the aroma of mulled wine, and the sound of carols. You can enjoy local delicacies, handmade crafts, and live music while absorbing the sights and sounds of the season.

Discover ancient customs that have been passed down through generations, such as the Scottish Hogmanay, a lively multi-day festival celebrating the New Year with unique traditions like the First Footing, where the first person to cross your door brings good luck for the coming year.

Embark on a journey through time as you witness medieval reenactments and historical parades that bring the past to life, offering a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage that shapes Europe’s identity and heritage.

Key Takeaways:

  • Christmas markets offer a unique and festive atmosphere, with local delicacies, handmade crafts, and live music.
  • Traditional celebrations like the Scottish Hogmanay showcase ancient customs and bring communities together.
  • Historical reenactments and parades provide a glimpse into Europe’s rich cultural heritage and history.

Stunning Winter Landscapes: A Wanderer’s Guide to Europe in December#

For a European getaway bathed in the ethereal beauty of winter and its snowy embrace, December is arguably the most enchanting time to visit. From the snowy charm of the Austrian Alps to the glistening canals of Amsterdam, Europe transforms into a picture-perfect winter wonderland, beckoning travelers with its unique festive spirit and endless opportunities for exploration.

The Magic of the Austrian Alps:#

When the snow-covered mountains beckon, the Austrian Alps emerge as a true winter paradise. Picture yourself gliding down pristine slopes, inhaling the crisp alpine air and marveling at the picturesque scenery. Whether you’re a seasoned skier or a beginner, the region’s world-class resorts cater to all levels, offering an unforgettable skiing experience. And after a day on the slopes, cozy up in a traditional chalet, sipping on warm mulled wine by the crackling fireplace—pure bliss!

A Winter Fairytale in the Heart of Europe:#

Embark on a romantic sojourn to Prague, a city that exudes timeless charm during the winter season. Stroll through the Old Town Square, adorned with twinkling lights and festive decorations, and immerse yourself in the magic of the Christmas markets. Revel in the aromas and flavors of traditional Czech delicacies, sip on steaming cups of mulled wine, and find unique treasures among the handcrafted souvenirs.

Experience Amsterdam’s Winter Delights:#

Glide along the frozen canals of Amsterdam, a city that takes on a magical allure in winter. Whether you opt for a leisurely boat tour or prefer to strap on your skates and join the locals on the ice, the city’s canals offer a unique and enchanting way to explore. And as the sun sets, the city transforms into a captivating winter wonderland, with twinkling lights illuminating the waterways and bridges, creating an unforgettable spectacle.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Winter in Europe: Embrace the beauty of winter in Europe, where snow-covered landscapes and festive cheer create a magical atmosphere.

  • Austrian Alps: Indulge in world-class skiing, relish traditional Austrian cuisine, and relax in cozy chalets amidst breathtaking alpine scenery.

  • Prague’s Winter Charm: Wander through Prague’s Old Town Square, adorned with Christmas decorations, and savor the flavors of traditional Czech delicacies at the festive markets.

  • Amsterdam’s Frozen Canals: Experience the enchantment of skating or cruising along Amsterdam’s frozen canals, surrounded by twinkling lights and festive decorations.

FAQ#

Q1: What are the best European cities to visit in December for Christmas markets?#

A1: Strasbourg, Germany; Vienna, Austria; Cologne, Germany; Prague, Czech Republic; and Budapest, Hungary are renowned for their festive Christmas markets, offering unique shopping experiences and delightful treats.

Q2: Can you recommend some lesser-known European destinations suitable for winter sports enthusiasts?#

A2: For skiing and snowboarding, consider the slopes of Andorra, Bulgaria, or Georgia. For breathtaking winter landscapes and cross-country skiing, explore the valleys of Slovenia or the mountains of Romania.

Q3: Where in Europe can I witness the Northern Lights in December?#

A3: Head to the Arctic Circle destinations like Lapland in Finland, Tromso in Norway, or Abisko National Park in Sweden for the best chances of seeing the magical Northern Lights.

Q4: Are there any unique cultural events or festivals happening in Europe in December?#

A4: Attend the Winter Festival of Lights in Lyon, France, for stunning light displays or experience the traditional Christmas Eve dinner known as “Nochebuena” in Madrid, Spain.

Q5: What are some tips for planning a European trip in December?#

A5: Plan in advance to secure accommodations and transportation, pack warm clothing and accessories, check for any visa requirements, and consider travel insurance to protect yourself against unexpected events.
