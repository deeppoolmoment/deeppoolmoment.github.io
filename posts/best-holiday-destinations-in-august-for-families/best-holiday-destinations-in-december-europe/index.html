Best Holiday Destinations in December Europe#

As the snow falls and the air turns crisp, Europe transforms into a magical winter wonderland, beckoning travelers with its festive charm and enchanting holiday experiences. From the twinkling lights of Christmas markets to the snow-capped mountains perfect for skiing, Europe offers a myriad of destinations that promise an unforgettable holiday experience.

Festive Markets#

The best holiday destinations in December Europe wouldn’t be complete without a visit to its renowned Christmas markets. Stroll through the wooden stalls adorned with twinkling lights, and fill your senses with the aromas of mulled wine, roasted chestnuts, and gingerbread. Discover unique handmade gifts, sip on traditional beverages, and indulge in delectable festive treats as you soak up the joyous atmosphere.

Snowy Landscapes#

For those seeking a winter adventure, Europe’s snowy landscapes offer a breathtaking backdrop for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports. Glide down pristine slopes, surrounded by stunning mountain scenery, and enjoy the exhilaration of carving through fresh powder. Afterward, cozy up in a charming chalet, sip on a warm drink, and let the beauty of nature soothe your soul.

Cultural Experiences#

Europe’s rich cultural heritage comes alive during the holiday season, offering unique opportunities to immerse yourself in local traditions and festivities. Visit centuries-old cathedrals adorned with elaborate Christmas decorations, attend traditional concerts featuring festive music, or witness enchanting light displays that transform cities into glittering masterpieces.

Top Destinations#

  • Finland: Experience the magic of Lapland, where you can meet Santa Claus, go on a reindeer sleigh ride, and witness the mesmerizing Northern Lights.

  • Austria: Discover the charm of Vienna, where you can explore the iconic Christmas markets, skate on frozen lakes, and indulge in traditional Austrian delicacies.

  • Germany: Immerse yourself in the festive spirit of Nuremberg, renowned for its Christkindlesmarkt, one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in Germany.

  • France: Experience the grandeur of Paris illuminated with holiday lights, visit the enchanting Christmas market at the Champs-Élysées, and take a leisurely stroll along the Seine River.

  • Italy: Discover the beauty of Rome during the holiday season, where you can explore the Vatican City, marvel at the festive decorations, and indulge in traditional Italian cuisine.

While the best holiday destinations in December Europe offer a plethora of experiences, there are a few practical considerations to keep in mind:

  • Weather: December in Europe can be cold and snowy, so pack warm clothing and layers to stay comfortable.

  • Crowds: Tourist destinations can be crowded during the holiday season, so consider booking accommodations and transportation in advance.

  • Prices: Prices for flights, accommodations, and activities may be higher during the peak holiday season.

  • Plan Your Itinerary: With so much to see and do, it’s essential to plan your itinerary and prioritize your must-see attractions to make the most of your trip.

  • Enjoy the Festive Spirit: Embrace the magic of the holiday season, participate in local traditions, and enjoy the unique experiences that Europe offers during this time of year.

Top activities and attractions for a зимняя wonderland experience#

We all crave a bit of icy magic during the holiday season. Picture this: snow-covered landscapes, twinkling lights, and festive markets filled with mulled wine and gingerbread aromas. Well, Europe in December is your ultimate зимняя wonderland. Come along as we unveil the best holiday destinations for a truly unforgettable experience.

1. Discover the Festive Charm of Prague, Czech Republic:#

The Czech capital transforms into a winter wonderland during December. Visit the iconic Old Town Square, where you’ll find a massive Christmas market with wooden stalls selling handmade crafts, ornaments, and local delicacies. Be sure to try the Trdelnik, a sweet pastry that’s roasted over an open fire. Don’t miss the Astronomical Clock Show, where the 12 apostles parade every hour.

2. Experience Enchantment in Rovaniemi, Finland - Santa’s Official Home:#

If you’ve ever dreamed of meeting Santa Claus, head to Rovaniemi, Finland. This is the official hometown of the jolly old elf, and you can visit his office, meet his reindeer, and even send a letter from Santa’s post office. Take a reindeer or husky safari through the snowy forests, and don’t forget to try some traditional Finnish dishes like reindeer meat stew.

3. Admire the Alpine Beauty of Grindelwald, Switzerland:#

Nestled amidst the Swiss Alps, Grindelwald is a picturesque village that exudes winter charm. Skiing, snowboarding, and tobogganing are must-do activities here. Take the cable car up to Mount First and witness stunning panoramic views of the snow-capped mountains. Treat yourself to a fondue dinner at a cozy mountaintop restaurant.

4. Immerse in the Christmas Spirit of Budapest, Hungary:#

Budapest is a captivating city any time of the year, but it truly shines during December. Stroll through the bustling Christmas markets along Vaci Street and Vorosmarty Square. Admire the city’s landmarks, like the Hungarian Parliament Building and Buda Castle, beautifully illuminated for the holidays. Indulge in Hungarian delicacies like goulash and langos while sipping on mulled wine.

5. Witness the Magic of Colmar, France:#

Colmar, a medieval town in the Alsace region of France, is a fairytale come to life during the holiday season. Explore the charming streets adorned with festive decorations, visit the enchanting Christmas markets, and admire the colorful half-timbered houses. Don’t miss the opportunity to try Alsatian specialties like tarte flambée (a type of flatbread) and bredele (traditional Christmas cookies).

Unique cultural experiences during the holiday season#

Europe is an incredible place to visit during the holiday season. From festive markets to enchanting winter landscapes, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Here are a few of the best places to go in December:

Strasbourg, France#

Strasbourg is celebrated for its unique cultural experiences during the holiday season. The city is adorned with twinkling lights, and the air is filled with the scent of mulled wine and roasted chestnuts. Be sure to visit the Christkindelsmärik, one of the oldest Christmas markets in Europe. You’ll find everything from traditional Alsatian food to handmade gifts.

Nuremberg, Germany#

Nuremberg is another great place to experience the magic of Christmas. The city’s famous Christkindlesmarkt dates back to the 16th century and features over 180 stalls selling everything you need for a festive holiday. You can also enjoy a ride on the Nuremberg Children’s Railway, or take a stroll through the Old Town.

Copenhagen, Denmark#

Copenhagen is a winter wonderland during the holiday season. The city is covered in a blanket of snow, and the canals are frozen over, and the Tivoli Gardens are transformed into a winter wonderland with twinkling lights, Christmas trees, and ice skating rinks.

Prague, Czech Republic#

Prague is a beautiful city to visit any time of year, but it’s especially magical during the holiday season. The Old Town Square is home to a large Christmas market, and there are festive decorations all over the city. Be sure to try some of the traditional Czech Christmas dishes, such as roast goose and potato salad.

Vienna, Austria#

Vienna is a city that knows how to celebrate the holidays. The city is home to several Christmas markets, including the famous Christkindlmarkt on Rathausplatz. You can also enjoy a concert at the Vienna State Opera or take a carriage ride through the Old Town.
